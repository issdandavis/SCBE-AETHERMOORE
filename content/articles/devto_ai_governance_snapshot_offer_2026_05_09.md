Most teams do not need a giant AI governance program on day one.

They need a first map.

If you are using AI in a client workflow, internal automation, support tool, data pipeline, or customer-facing feature, the early questions are practical:

- What data enters the system?
- What does the model influence?
- What outputs are shown to users or staff?
- What gets logged?
- What would happen if the prompt, vendor, or workflow failed?
- What evidence would a customer, partner, or reviewer expect?

I opened a fixed-scope **AI Governance Snapshot** for exactly that first pass.

## What the snapshot covers

The review is scoped to one AI workflow, toolchain, or customer-facing AI feature.

You get:

- a 2-page findings memo
- three prioritized fixes
- a practical evidence checklist
- follow-up steps

No secrets or production credentials are needed for the first pass. Sanitized screenshots, public docs, architecture notes, and workflow descriptions are enough to start.

## Who it is for

This fits:

- founders using AI in client workflows
- small teams shipping AI features
- subcontract leads who need a governance read
- operators who need a plain-English risk map before spending weeks on policy work

It is not a replacement for legal advice, certification, or full enterprise audit. It is the first structured review before the workflow grows roots.
