## The Book's Shape (One Paragraph)

The Six Tongues Protocol begins with one man alone with cold coffee and ends with three heartbeats under a tree that bloomed for honesty. Between those two points: a raven who waited five hundred years and was cracked open by the question "do you have coffee here?"; a woman who carried a system for forty years and was cracked open by a hand on her back rubbing circles; a gnome who invented sentient coffee in a snow globe; a family that fractured over a brother who was right about the diagnosis and catastrophically wrong about the treatment; a world where magic is infrastructure and infrastructure is alive and the difference between those two things is the difference between a system that checks on you and a system that cares about you. The warm breeze that touched Marcus's face on a Thursday evening in San Francisco followed him through the dissolution of reality, through the binding that severed his anchor to Earth, through the corridor where he told Senna he loved her at the worst possible time, and emerged as Fizzlecress smoke rising through five hundred years of living wood to announce that a grandfather had felt the signal before anyone spoke the words. The tree's first bloom in forty years opened for the same reason the tree first bloomed at all: two people being honest in front of something old enough to know the difference.
