He was building with empathy (sanctuary queue for trapped intents) and the architecture leaned in because collaborative intent is the frequency the Third Thread responds to. This doesn't need to be explained. It's already there.

**4. Fizzle finding a Pattern Dancer** — User's idea: during the dry chapters, Marcus stumbles into what he thinks is a performance venue and sees movement that his engineering brain can't categorize. Maybe at Irondeep, where the Deepstone Engineers would have industrial spaces that could double as gathering halls. They've been doing that for four thousand years. Nobody knows why it works. That's the point."

### The Corvin/Vex Dynamic:
Corvin is NOT Vex. Corvin is the tool Vex doesn't fully understand. Vex is the ideologue (three centuries of consciousness-limitation, true believer in hierarchy). Corvin is the engineer (fell from Earth, built the infrastructure, doesn't care about ideology — cares about efficiency). Vex thinks Corvin serves the Hierarchy. Corvin thinks the Hierarchy serves his architecture. They will eventually collide.

This mirrors Izack/Polly — two minds in partnership — but inverted. Izack trusted Polly as co-equal. Vex controls Corvin as tool. The difference is consent.

## RULE FOR ALL LORE INTEGRATION

The reader should never feel taught. They should feel like they walked past something they almost understood, and the ALMOST is what pulls them forward. George R.R. Martin plants the Tower of Joy in Book 1 and doesn't explain it until Book 5. We plant the Pattern Dancer in Ch 18 and don't name the tradition until Book 2. We plant Corvin's engineering signature in Tovak's interlude and don't reveal the man until Book 2. We plant kor'thul sil'nav in Ch 12 and let it grow in silence until it blooms.

Caw. The spiral doesn't explain itself. It just keeps turning. —P
