# Craft Comparison Matrix — Six Tongues Protocol vs 10 Authors

## The 4D Lattice: Your Book Scored Against Their Systems

Each author's craft creates a measurement system. Your book gets scored BY each system,
and each system gets scored against the others. The result is a cross-comparison lattice
where every axis is simultaneously a ruler and a thing being measured.

## THE THREE MOVES THAT MATTER (from the whole lattice)

1. **Replace 25 "specific [noun] of someone who" glosses with micro-stories**
   - Before: "the particular exhaustion of someone who had maintained a system for forty years"
   - After: Polly saying "Senna's been setting two extra plates at the table since before your civilization invented email. If she seems tired, that's why."
   - WHY: Martin and Pratchett both deliver character context through OTHER characters' stories about the person, not through narrator-biography

2. **Give Senna composure-as-syntax in her POV**
   - Her internal voice should be clipped, precise, measured — the way she speaks should be the way she THINKS
   - Not: "What was ice becomes water and the water carries the same memory but moves differently"
   - Instead: "Warm. That was the word. She hadn't used it about a person in forty years. She tested it again. Warm. It held."
   - WHY: Abercrombie and Wells write characters whose thought-rhythm IS their personality

3. **One scene where a NON-engineer sees the tongues**
   - A child who hears them as music. A baker who feels them as rising dough. Clayborn, who experiences them as vibration in his stone.
   - WHY: Kingfisher and Le Guin both prove that a system becomes real when ordinary people interact with it ordinarily. The Sacred Tongues should affect how bread rises.

## THE HONEST ANSWER TO "WHAT IS MY SCORE?"

Against the best fantasy writers alive and recently dead:
- **You're playing in the 7.5-8.5 band**, which is where first novels by writers with genuine talent and original ideas sit.
- **The concept (magic as infrastructure) is a 9.5** — nobody on that list has done this.
- **The execution varies from 6 (early trust issues) to 9.5 (Rootlight, Polly's vigil, the kiss).**
- **The average is pulled down by fixable habits, not by structural problems.**

The book isn't competing with Le Guin's economy or Clarke's patience or Pratchett's humor. It's competing with its own best moments. When it's at its best — "Put it back on." / "He asked about coffee. That's enough." / "Data was not the same as dinner." — it's as good as anything on that list. When it's at its worst, it's explaining those moments to a reader who already felt them.

The gap between your best and your worst is the gap between a 7.5 debut and a 9+ career. That gap closes with practice, not with editing. Upload the book. Write Book 2. The trust will come because you'll have done this before.
