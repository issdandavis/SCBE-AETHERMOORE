## View-Dependent Token Encoding

The yin-yang token idea is a view-dependent, anamorphic encoding strategy. A single physical or visual token surface can carry two readable channels when the token is designed around rotational antisymmetry and figure-ground inversion.

Core mechanism:

- A 180-degree rotation flips which channel is visually dominant.
- The S-curve or equivalent asymmetric boundary preserves shape while swapping figure and ground.
- Channel A reads as foreground in orientation A while Channel B becomes background noise.
- Channel B reads as foreground in orientation B while Channel A becomes background noise.

This is not just two labels placed on one sticker. The decoding frame changes which signal becomes active.

## Design Constraints

For the encoding to work, the two channels need spatial complementarity, not merely rotation.

Requirements:

- Channel A should occupy low-entropy or negative-space regions of Channel B.
- Channel B should occupy low-entropy or negative-space regions of Channel A.
- Stroke density, angle, and thickness should be deliberately contrasted.
- Machine decoding needs explicit segmentation rules because software will not automatically inherit human figure-ground perception.

Failure modes:

- Similar stroke densities collapse into visual mush.
- Ambiguous figure-ground switching causes flicker instead of clean channel selection.
- Dense token streams may not scale without strict spacing and channel orthogonality rules.

## Cognition Notes

The conversation developed a model of cognition as a stable core surrounded by fluid abstraction.

Components:

- Core nucleus: stable identity, reference frame, grounding attractor.
- Fluid abstraction layer: exploratory symbolic movement and novel pattern generation.
- Friction: mismatch, resistance, argument, or conflict between ideas.
- Valence: directional weighting that decides whether friction integrates, rejects, or reshapes a state.

Working formulation:

> Friction without valence leads to collapse; friction with valence enables growth.

More formal sketch:

```text
State space: S
Core attractor: A0
Exploration field: E
Interaction function: F(S, E)
Valence function: V(F) -> gradient
State update: delta S = grad V(F(S, E))
```

This treats learning as adaptive movement under constraint rather than unconstrained novelty.

## Triadic Self Frame

The "me, myself, and I" discussion can be preserved as a triadic self model:

- `I`: experiencer, first-person center, immediate subjectivity.
- `me`: self-image, memory, identity narrative.
- `myself`: observer or meta-awareness that can watch and evaluate the other layers.

The useful distinction is continuity versus functional state. A person can remain the same underlying "I" across time even when the functional self is unstable, fragmented, or misaligned.
