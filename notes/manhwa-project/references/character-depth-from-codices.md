# Character Depth — Drawn from the Seven Codices

A working layer on top of `visual-persona-sheet.md` and `character-catalog.md`. The persona sheet handles *how the character moves through a panel*. This file handles *what they're carrying when they walk in*.

Two source layers feed every entry:

- **Bright Codex** — *The Complete Avalon Project Archive: Seven Sacred Codices*. Founding-era voice, mentor mannerisms, the laughter that built the world.
- **Dark Codex** — *The Avalon Codex: A Multi-Generational Epic*. Four generations of betrayal, war, frozen-king cost. The bill the world is still paying.

The new book sits late in the timeline — the bright codex is *bones* (childhood memory, founding myths everyone learned in school), the dark codex is *scars* (the actual cost the empire is now buckling under). Together they give every character a **public face** and a **private wound**.

Each entry uses three fields:

- **Bones** — bright-codex inheritance. Voice habits, signature moves, the founding-era shape they grew up inside.
- **Scar** — dark-codex cost. A specific named loss. Not a mood; a person, a date, a decision.
- **Tell** — how the scar leaks through the bones in the present-day book.

## Izack Thorne

- **Bones.** Founder. Talks to rocks and snapdragons; treats every magical object as a conversation partner. "Magic is a conversation, not a conquest." Tendency to get lost in a problem so deep he forgets to eat. Bright-codex Izack is curious, methodical, gently humorous, and apologizes to plants. He greeted Malzeth'irun with "I think you'll find we specialize in second chances here." (Seven Codices, Vignettes II, IV.)
- **Scar.** *The Ides of Convergence.* Betrayed by his own inner circle at the Year-25 Sundering. They thought he'd gone too far. He used the Transdimensional Robes to scatter Avalon across infinite realities and anchored the network with his own life. Did not die so much as become load-bearing. (Multi-Gen, Gen-1 climax.)
- **Tell.** He doesn't look up. The room around him does the carrying. When his pipe smoke curls gentler, *that's* the emotional weather report. He won't take a posture he can't hold for another five hundred years.

## Polly

- **Bones.** Full title: *Polydimensional Manifestation of Accumulated Wisdom and Occasional Sarcasm.* Met Izack arguing with sedimentary rock; chose him for asking "Would you like to help me figure out what I'm doing wrong?" Sarcasm-as-essential-moral-support. Authored the *Wingscrolls.* "Magic is just the universe's way of having a sense of humor. The trick is learning to laugh along." (Seven Codices, Vignettes I, VI, IX.)
- **Scar.** Her *true nature revealed* at the Battle for Archive Prime — she **is** the living Codex Eternis. Cardinal Polly diplomatic phase; centuries of being the only one who remembers what was actually said in the founding rooms. Watched Izack become the wall. (Multi-Gen, Gen-2.)
- **Tell.** Her wit is older than her witticisms. She uses longer constructions for sarcasm because sarcasm needs architecture. The sleep-caw next to Marcus is the most important sound in the book — she has decided this spot is safe enough to close her eyes for the first time in centuries.

## Aria Ravencrest Thorne

- **Bones.** Met Izack on a Tuesday. Boundary-mage scholar of House Ravencrest. He recontextualized her entire magical practice in thirty seconds and she invited him to dinner. "A true boundary doesn't just keep danger out. It creates a space where good things can grow." Direct, fierce, protective; *diplomatically ruthless* to anyone who threatens her people. Few words, all chosen. (Seven Codices, Codex IV; Vignette II.)
- **Scar.** *The Ravencrest Betrayal.* Discovers her own father, Count Eldrin, was a double agent funding the Dominion Academy. Eldrin redeems himself dying to save her at the Siege of Seven Spires. Aria carries both his treason and his redemption as a single mass. (Multi-Gen, Gen-1.)
- **Tell.** Refuses to let anyone mythologize Izack's sacrifice. She was there. She is precise about what it cost. Her hand on the World Tree bark gets a warmer answer than anyone else's because it is the same hand that lived through the betrayal — the tree remembers.

## Alexander Thorne

- **Bones.** Born during magical aurora; every bell in Avalon rang untouched at his birth; his first sound harmonized with the bells. As a baby his fingers traced dimensional-magic runic syntax in the air. Bright-codex Alexander is *gentle wisdom beyond his years, natural teacher, instinctive peacemaker.* "Every being wants to be understood. Magic just helps us speak the same language." (Seven Codices, Vignette VI; Codex IV.)
- **Scar.** *The Iron Mask.* Caelynn Brightwater, the love of his life, assassinated by Dominion zealots during childbirth. He becomes "The Winter King" — never shows emotion publicly again. Later orders the public execution of Lyra Netweave (Zara's daughter) for treason. Eventually loses his humanity merging into the World Tree network. (Multi-Gen, Gen-2.)
- **Tell.** The Izack-adjacent smile. Asking "how are you finding it?" instead of "how do you feel about the existential weight." He chose the outer ring not because he didn't care, but because he cared enough to know his grief would go structural if he stayed close.

## Zara Millwright

- **Bones.** Arrived through a dimensional rift at sixteen, Year 3. Dragon-kin heritage, Third Thread catalyst bloodline. Fell in love with Alexander's mind one lesson at a time — "I don't understand the Third Thread theory… the math works, but it feels incomplete." "What do you think is missing?" Brilliant teacher who pushes students past their own expectations. "Power without purpose is just destruction waiting to happen." (Seven Codices, Vignette VII; Codex IV.)
- **Scar.** *The Wedding Massacre.* Marcus Joren Brennan — her childhood friend, battlefield partner, fiancé — dies in her arms during their wedding, killed by Dominion assassins. Their unborn child is lost in the magical feedback. Grief becomes combat fury. She is "The Widow General" forever after. (Multi-Gen, Gen-1.)
- **Tell.** Defensive walls go up before warmth does. Her teaching style is *push them past their own expectations* because she had to be pushed past hers by Marcus's death. She believes potential is what you fight forward into when the universe has taken everything else.

## Senna Thorne

- **Bones.** Youngest of Izack and Aria's children. Has her father's jaw, her brother Alexander's stillness, her mother's ability to make a decision before anyone else knows there's a decision to make. The bright-codex inheritance is the family habit of *setting the table for everyone, including the absent.* Companion Phasewing, the whisper-fox of integration. Opaline sacred egg.
- **Scar.** Has been the load-bearing wall since Kael was erased and Alexander took the perimeter. Forty years of carrying everyone, including the ones who are no longer in the room. She wakes at 4:47 AM and stopped attending Rootlight for years because there was too much family memory in the light. (Visual persona sheet + dark-codex inheritance pattern — the Izack lineage's signature is *loving a structure by becoming part of it*.)
- **Tell.** Left hand adjusts right cuff. That cuff adjustment is the leak in the armor. If she stops managing herself, the scene is serious. Her composure cracks not into vulnerability but into the person the composure was built to protect.

## Bram Cortez — CA Maintenance Lead

- **Bones.** Cassisivadan tongue (CA), the Root Network guild — ecological communion, gardener-engineer, *play is the highest form of intelligence.* The bright-codex CA inheritance is the gardener Sarah Lin Chen ("builds things that grow rather than things that dominate") combined with the Fizzle Brightcog engineer ("complex magical machinery, student project assistance"). He treats infrastructure like a living thing because in the founding myth, infrastructure was alive.
- **Scar.** Twenty-three years on the maintenance crew. Has seen the empire's bones crack from the inside; has personally welded patches over wounds the policy class refuses to name. *"Chaos is free and structure is labor"* — his thesis, paid for in unpaid overtime.
- **Tell.** Anger gets quieter, not louder. Sparse words because one word might be misinterpreted and he doesn't have time for ambiguity in load-bearing conversations. His tenderness comes out in how he touches a wall — gently, precisely, like something alive that deserves care.

## Nadia Kest — AV Relay Technician

- **Bones.** Avali tongue (AV), the Bridge Walkers guild — diplomacy, cross-cultural understanding, *hopeful openness.* Inherits Jennifer Elara Santos's pattern from the bright codex (eloquent, empathetic, sees multiple perspectives simultaneously, builds bridges). The body-clock synchronized to the Protocol's 0.3-second heartbeat is the next-generation version of "the kind of teacher who makes the extraordinary feel survivable by treating it as a Tuesday."
- **Scar.** She is the team member who *runs* — when she runs the reader feels the urgency. Carrying that pace for years means she's never been allowed the slow grief the others get. She is the one who keeps the room alive while everyone else processes; she rarely gets to be the one who needs the room.
- **Tell.** Shows up at the festival with two drinks. Treats apocalypse as a Tuesday. Says what everyone else is thinking before they know they were thinking it. The festival drinks count is her recovery margin.

## Sera Voss — RU Adjudicator

- **Bones.** Runethic tongue (RU), the Oath Keepers guild — ancestral memory, oaths that bind across generations, *historical preservation as moral discipline.* Inherits Elena Mara Vasquez's pattern from the bright codex (methodical, brilliant, quietly confident, approaches magic like mathematics). Silver hair braided with policy tags is the visible form of the inheritance.
- **Scar.** Has spent her career calibrating the distance between fairness and feeling because *someone has to.* The Runethic discipline she enforces is also the discipline she is trapped inside — the same oaths that hold the empire together hold her in place.
- **Tell.** Flatness as characterization, not absence. Every sentence is measured. When her warmth appears, it is *one degree of temperature change* — the attentive will notice, the careless will miss it. That one degree is her entire emotional range in public.

## Tovak Rel — UM Specialist

- **Bones.** Umbroth tongue (UM), the Shadow Court guild — concealment, productive discontinuity, *some truths require darkness to remain true.* No direct bright-codex analogue — Tovak is mostly dark-codex inheritance. He represents the part of Avalon that never made it into the founding myths because the founding myths require visibility, and intelligence work requires its opposite.
- **Scar.** Saw the three Dominion handlers around Kael for fourteen months before they dissolved simultaneously. He reported it. The Council filed the report and moved on. He did not report again, but he did not stop watching. The cost of being right about Kael alone in a room of people who weren't ready to hear it.
- **Tell.** Three words per conversation, average. When he speaks at length, the room goes quiet. Operates from the negative space — what's *absent*, not what's present. The patience of someone who has learned that premature disclosure compromises both the intelligence and the source.

## Jorren Hale — DR Archivist

- **Bones.** Draumric tongue (DR), the Forge Masters guild — structure, manifestation, *what you build you defend.* Inherits Sarah Lin Chen's bright-codex pattern of *systems-focused, sees magic as part of natural cycles, builds things that grow.* The leather ledger is his anvil; record-keeping is his forge work.
- **Scar.** *"I keep records and grudges in equal order."* The DR discipline is the tongue that refuses to let the system forget what it did. Jorren is the institutional memory of every wrong the empire has not yet apologized for, and the empire is now declining under exactly that unpaid debt.
- **Tell.** Quiet, precise, dry as old paper. Square spectacles. When he opens the ledger, the conversation has just become permanent.

## Lyra Thorne (Probability Researcher)

- **Bones.** Emerald sacred egg, companion Quiris (spider-lizard of curiosity). Daughter of Izack and Aria. Bright-codex inheritance: the Maya Seren Brightwater pattern (diplomatic, thoughtful, natural synthesizer, the Academy's answer to complex problems requiring multiple perspectives) combined with the family humor.
- **Scar.** Carries the *name* of Lyra Netweave — Zara's daughter from the dark codex, "The Iron Maiden of the Eastern Front," who betrayed Avalon (Benedict Arnold parallel) and was publicly executed by Alexander, breaking his heart. This Lyra is not that Lyra. But she shares the name, and the family carries the weight of how the *last* Lyra ended.
- **Tell.** Delivers truth disguised as banter at the worst possible moment. "The gate is not blushing. You are." Her quickness is partly inheritance and partly insurance — if she names things first, no one else gets to weaponize them.

## Mira Thorne (Verdant Tithe's Voice)

- **Bones.** Sapphire sacred egg, companion Whispenook (shadow-light bat of wisdom). Chose silence as a discipline. Bright-codex inheritance: the forest-keeper pattern, gentleness held to the point of becoming a scalpel.
- **Scar.** She understood the Verdant Tithe — the cost the forest pays to keep the realm together — *before the family did.* Carrying that knowledge alone for the years before anyone else caught up is its own quiet wound.
- **Tell.** Level voice that says things that sound gentle until you realize they were scalpels. "That can make people unusually teachable." "That is why it may help you." She names what others are avoiding. She does not raise her voice to do it.

## Kael Thorne (The Spiral-Walker)

- **Bones.** Prismatic sacred egg — the synthesis tongue, the seventh absence. Youngest son of Izack and Aria. Bright-codex inheritance: the family genius, the gentle Alexander-pattern. Bright-codex Avalon believed in second chances. Kael grew up inside that belief.
- **Scar.** Saw the gap in the system at fourteen — *the six tongues govern life but nothing governs death.* No one listened. By sixteen, three Dominion handlers had positioned themselves around him for fourteen months and dissolved simultaneously. Tovak reported it; the Council filed it. Kael was right about the gap and wrong about the architecture. He is *erased, not killed,* and his code is still running in the infrastructure — not as malware, but as unfinished work.
- **Tell.** Not heard directly in the present book. Present through his code, his philosophy, and how the room reacts when his name is spoken — Polly's feathers storm-flatten; Senna's jaw sets; Bram swears in Draumric. "Almost beautiful" is how his routing pattern reads. The fault he created is the empire's most expensive open ticket.

## Marcus Chen (the new arrival)

- **Bones.** Burned-out engineer, dropped through reality, asks "Would you like to help me figure out what I'm doing wrong?" — the exact line that won Polly five hundred years ago. Bright-codex inheritance through *resonance, not ancestry:* he carries Izack's intellectual register without knowing it.
- **Scar.** Sixteen-hour shifts, the apartment he didn't miss, the framework he believed more than he believed in any person. The dimensional offset that makes him valuable is the same thing that makes him cost more — his weakness and his purpose are the same phenomenon viewed from different angles.
- **Tell.** Humor as armor. Trusts frameworks faster than he trusts people, but once he trusts a person he builds load-bearing structures on them without realizing it. Stays not because he can't leave but because he *chose to.* That choice is the most important thing about him, and the empire is in trouble specifically because nobody else has been allowed to choose in three generations.

## How to use this file

- **In dialogue.** When a character speaks, ask: *is this the bright voice or the dark voice?* Most of the time it's the bright voice with the dark vocabulary leaking through. The Tell line is the leak.
- **In a fight or confrontation.** The Scar is the pressure point. Push on it and the bright voice cracks; the dark voice answers.
- **In a quiet moment.** The Bones are what they reach for when nothing is on fire — Izack apologizing to a snapdragon, Aria pulling something out of dirt, Polly making the same joke for the four-hundredth time because the joke is older than the room.
- **For a panel.** The persona sheet tells you the body. This file tells you the *thought just before the body moves.*

Two-line rule for new scenes: if you don't know which Bone they came in with and which Scar is being pressed, write one more sentence of staging before the next line of dialogue.
