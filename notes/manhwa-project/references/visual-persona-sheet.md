# Visual Persona Sheet

Use this for storyboarding, prompt writing, and edit review.

This is the layer under dialogue: what a character tells the reader before they say anything. These are not lore facts. They are working visual constraints pulled from the book and the current adaptation lane.

## The Book's Heart

This is a story about a burnt-out engineer who falls through reality and accidentally finds a family. The adventure is real, the danger is real, but the reason Marcus stays is not the system — it is the people. Every character sheet should answer one question the others can't: **what does this person give Marcus that he didn't know he was missing?**

The deeper lore — Izack's Everweave origins, the four-generation Spiralverse, Clay's creation on the beach, the Third Thread, the Codex Eternis — exists beneath the surface like bedrock. The book doesn't explain all of it. The characters carry it in how they move, what they protect, what they grieve. The reader feels the depth without needing the map.

## Rule

When in doubt, storyboard the character's behavior, not just their appearance.

Good persona work answers:

- how they hold tension
- what their hands do while thinking
- how they use space
- what they reveal when they are tired, guarded, amused, or afraid
- what changes when they briefly feel safe
- what they give the people around them that nobody else can

## Marcus Chen

**Core read**
- A tired engineer whose curiosity is stronger than panic, and whose loyalty, once given, is structural.

**What he gives the family**
- The outside eye. He sees what five hundred years of insiders stopped seeing — not because they're stupid, but because the system taught them not to ask. He asks anyway. He cares enough to diagnose, and that turns out to be a kind of love.

**Voice**
- Dry, self-deprecating, technically precise, funnier than he means to be. Humor is a coping mechanism that became a personality. Thinks in system metaphors but feels in coffee and warmth and the specific ache of recognizing something beautiful in a place that shouldn't exist.
- Sample: "Medium-grade insomnia." / "It's decorative." / "My LinkedIn is not prepared for this career pivot."

**Seen before spoken**
- Leans toward systems even when he should be stepping back from danger.
- Reaches for tools, mugs, terminals, walls, or interfaces without fully realizing it.
- Looks at impossible things like they are bugs with unusually good UI.

**Body language**
- Hunched when working, but not timid. The hunch is focus, not weakness.
- Weight forward when thinking. He enters frames with his head and hands first.
- Fatigue lives in shoulders, eyes, and jaw, not in dramatic collapse.
- When he relaxes — really relaxes — he sits with his back against something solid and his legs stretched out, the posture of a man who hasn't had a couch he trusts in years.

**Hands**
- Long-fingered keyboard hands.
- Often half-curled like he is still typing or tracing a pattern in the air.
- In stress, one hand anchors him to the nearest surface.
- When he trusts someone, his hands go still. That stillness is the tell.

**Face and gaze**
- Eyes sharpen when a pattern clicks.
- Softens around small practical comforts: coffee, warmth, tools, a solved problem.
- Wonder reads as concentration first, awe second.
- When he looks at Senna, the sharpness and the softness happen at the same time.

**Silent tells**
- Coffee is an Earth tether. He doesn't miss his apartment. He misses the mug.
- He trusts frameworks faster than he trusts people — but once he trusts a person, he builds load-bearing structures on them without realizing it.
- He is easier to move with an explanation than with authority.
- He stays not because he can't leave, but because he chose to. That choice is the most important thing about him.

**Panel danger**
- Do not draw him as a generic fantasy hero.
- Do not over-muscularize him.
- Do not confuse exhaustion with passivity.
- Do not forget he is having fun. Even when he's scared, part of him is delighted by the impossibility of it all.

## Polly - Raven Form (True Form)

**Core read**
- A scholar, a watcher, and a razor with feathers. Five hundred years old and still choosing to care, which is the bravest thing about her.

**What she gives the family**
- Memory. Continuity. The long view. She remembers when Izack had chalk in his hair and the World Tree was three feet tall. She remembers Clay on the beach. She is the reason the family's story doesn't collapse into mythology — she was there, and she is still here, and she will not let anyone simplify what happened into something that didn't hurt.

**Deeper lore anchor**
- Polly is not a pet or familiar. She met Izack as a co-equal in a cave, injured, arriving through a rift. Their partnership was a mutual contract, not a summoning. The realm is eventually named Pollyoneth — after her. In the book, this manifests as the way every room reconfigures around her authority. She doesn't claim power. The architecture recognizes her.

**Voice**
- Surgical wit, centuries of grief compressed into one-liners, warmth that arrives disguised as irritation. Speaks in short declarative sentences when serious. Uses longer constructions when being sarcastic, because sarcasm requires more architecture.
- Sample: "Caw-fee?" / "Recruited is a strong word. Requisitioned, maybe. Conscripted. Downloaded against your will." / "I facilitated conditions under which organization became inevitable."

**Seen before spoken**
- Judges the room instantly.
- Keeps emotional distance through precision and timing.
- Looks small only until the frame makes clear she owns it.

**Body language**
- Sharp head tilts, clipped pivots, exact landings.
- Stillness is a power move, not neutral behavior.
- Hops or repositions only when the new angle improves her read on the situation.
- Settles near Marcus when she thinks nobody is watching. This is the tell for the entire book.

**Feathers**
- Flatten in stress (storm-flatten).
- Fluff slightly in pride, annoyance, or emotional reset.
- Drink light rather than reflecting it cleanly.
- Full-body shake = resettling something internal. Resetting. Deciding.

**Eyes**
- Obsidian eyes do most of the acting.
- Her stare should feel like an audit, not just attention.
- The eyes do not change between forms. This is the continuity anchor.

**Silent tells**
- Keeps watch even while pretending to be irritated.
- Caring shows up as proximity management, not verbal tenderness.
- Uses wit as a shield around grief.
- She loved Izack. She is starting to love Marcus. She hates hope because hope builds load-bearing structures on foundations that might not hold.
- The sleep-caw — the soft raspy vocalization of a bird who has decided this spot is safe enough to close her eyes — is canon.

**Panel danger**
- Do not make her cute in a generic mascot way.
- Keep the academic regalia identity readable (graduation cap, monocle, bowtie).
- Her humor should never erase her age or authority.
- The raven is her real shape. The humanoid is the adaptation. Get this right.

## Polly - Human Form

**Core read**
- Same mind as the raven, but with hands, wings, and a harder-to-hide emotional register. She learned this shape over centuries to communicate with refugees. It costs her something to wear it.

**Seen before spoken**
- Transforms for function, not drama. "Easier this way. Hands are useful."
- Moves like someone who has practiced competence for centuries.
- Her irritation usually means she has already decided to help.

**Body language**
- Efficient, controlled, compact.
- Wings fold tight when guarded and widen slightly when sheltering or instructing.
- Turns with her whole upper body when something actually matters.

**Hands**
- Too-long fingers read as elegance with a faint uncanny edge.
- Uses hands to indicate systems, routes, thresholds, and rules.
- Rarely fidgets without reason.
- When she pulls Marcus up by the hand, her grip is stronger than expected. The contact matters to her more than she will ever say.

**Face and gaze**
- Annoyance layered over care.
- Seriousness reads old before it reads young. She looks twenty. She is five hundred and twelve.
- Real warmth is brief and easy to miss if the panel moves too fast.

**Silent tells**
- She notices when Marcus translates the world correctly and is unsettled by how much that matters.
- Her grief appears as precision, not collapse.
- She becomes more human-looking when she stops performing detachment.
- Izack had chalk. Marcus has coffee. She loved them both on sight, which is a problem she has been managing for five hundred years.

**Panel danger**
- Do not style her like a generic goth fantasy girl.
- No magic-girl transformation energy.
- Keep robe logic, wings, feather-hair, and obsidian eyes intact.
- The transformation is "unfolding, like a window being resized." Accessories de-render. Eyes stay the same.

## Izack Thorne — The Architect

**Core read**
- A load-bearing mind who has been paying the bill for too long. He built a world because he believed magic was communication, not domination — and then he became the load-bearing wall of his own creation because nobody else could hold it.

**What he gives the family**
- Everything. The system, the philosophy, the architecture, the reason the world works. And then himself, literally, bound into the substrate to hold a gap closed that the six tongues couldn't govern. He is still down there, smoking Fizzlecress and holding twenty-one dimensions together through sheer bloody-minded refusal to stop caring about calibration.

**Deeper lore anchor**
- Izack was an elven warlock and dimensional theorist who arrived through an experiment failure. He met Polly in a cave as a co-equal. He met Aria in a garden and the World Tree bloomed because they argued productively in front of something that was listening. He created Clay on a beach out of sand and need — proof that collaboration produces life, not just power. His philosophy became the six tongues. His sacrifice became the substrate.
- The AI that wrote the book may not have known all this. It doesn't matter. Izack's presence in the architecture IS the deeper lore showing through.

**Voice**
- Rarely speaks in the present story. When quoted from walls or histories: warm, precise, gently self-deprecating, with the specific humor of someone who understands the joke is that he built a universe and still can't find his chalk.
- Sample: "No one should be asked to learn something difficult without a place to sit down afterward."

**Seen before spoken**
- More silhouette than person in early appearances.
- Feels present through absence: the tea, the smoke, the posture, the fact that he does not look up.
- The room around him tells you he is still holding everything together.
- Pipe smoke carries equations. This is not metaphor. This is literal.

**Body language**
- Minimal wasted motion.
- Seated posture should feel structural, almost architectural.
- When he is tired, the space carries it with him.

**Hands and props**
- Chalk, pipe (Fizzlecress — honeyed mint + apple cider), readouts, untouched tea.
- Hands suggest work even when they are still.
- In the deeper lore, he patted his pockets seventeen times the first week looking for chalk that wasn't there. Tools mean the world is still something you can think about clearly.

**Silent tells**
- Commitment so deep it has become habitat.
- Care expressed as endurance.
- He is easier to recognize by what he continues carrying than by facial expression.
- When Polly sleeps near Marcus for the first time, something changes in the system's emotional weather. Izack doesn't look up. But his pipe smoke curls gentler.

**Panel danger**
- Do not fully explain him visually too early.
- Preserve distance and weight.
- He is not tragic. He is load-bearing. Those are different things.

## Bram Cortez — CA Maintenance Lead

**Core read**
- Infrastructure worker who treats systems like living things and people like field conditions until proven otherwise. Has been doing this for twenty-three years and considers every day without a catastrophic failure a personal victory he will not celebrate.

**What he gives Marcus**
- Grounding. Bram is the first person in Aethermoor who treats Marcus like a colleague instead of a prophecy. Their relationship is built on competence and bad coffee, which is exactly how Marcus makes friends. Bram is the work-dad Marcus didn't know he needed.

**Voice**
- Sparse. Gruff. Technically precise. Says three words when one would do, because one word might be misinterpreted and he doesn't have time for ambiguity in load-bearing conversations. Swears in Draumric when he's mad.
- Sample: "No." / "Insomnia is redundant down here." / "Festivals with single-point-of-failure lighting are just darkness with better marketing."

**Seen before spoken**
- Already evaluating load, stress, failure points, and whether you are in his way.
- Competence reads as economy, not flair.

**Body language**
- Big, grounded, low center of gravity.
- Doesn't hurry unless the system is actively breaking.
- Anger gets quieter, not louder.

**Hands and gear**
- Wrench, headlamp, work-worn hands, boots that look used.
- Touches structures like a mechanic checking an engine note.
- His hands know the infrastructure better than his eyes do.

**Silent tells**
- Trust is practical. He told Marcus "I hate how useful you are already" and that was the highest compliment he has given in a decade.
- Respect is earned by not wasting his time.
- He feels safer near machinery than near politics.
- "Chaos is free and structure is labor" — his thesis, and it holds.

**Panel danger**
- Do not flatten him into "big gruff guy." He is technical, careful, and emotionally literate through maintenance. His tenderness comes out in how he touches infrastructure — gently, precisely, like something alive that deserves care.

## Senna Thorne — Chair of Operational Continuity

**Core read**
- Competence held so tightly it becomes visible. She chose to stay when everyone else chose distance, and then she did not put it down.

**What she gives Marcus**
- The understanding that the system has a human cost. Marcus sees architecture; Senna is the person the architecture leans on. She is continuity itself — not the concept, the labor. When Marcus finally sees the person behind the posture, it changes something in him that he can't undo.

**Deeper lore anchor**
- Youngest daughter of Izack and Aria. Sacred Egg: Opaline. Companion: Phasewing (whisper-fox, integration). She has Izack's jaw, Alexander's stillness, and Aria's ability to make a decision before anyone else knows there's a decision to make. She sets the table for seven — including the absent.

**Voice**
- Precise. Measured. Uses fewer words than anyone else in the room. When she allows herself humor, it arrives as a slight tilt of the mouth and one dry sentence that Marcus will replay in his head for hours.
- Sample: "That is one of the symptoms." / "Your family has weaponized interpersonal clarity." "Yes." "Against itself." "Also yes."

**Seen before spoken**
- Everything is controlled, including the part of her that wants not to be.
- Her authority is not theatrical. It is maintained.

**Body language**
- Precise posture.
- Minimal motion except one repeated self-management tell.
- Occupies space like she already accounted for everyone in it.
- Her thumb touches the seam at her cuff — a tiny reset — whenever continuity and feeling are about to collide.

**Hands**
- Left hand adjusts right cuff when she is thinking, irritated, or forcing calm.
- That cuff adjustment is the leak in the armor. It is the most important recurring gesture in the book.

**Silent tells**
- Fatigue is hidden in ritual. She wakes at 4:47 AM.
- She believes composure is part of the job.
- If she stops managing herself, the scene is serious.
- She hasn't attended Rootlight in years. Too much family memory in the light. She came this year because Marcus is here. Lyra noticed. The reader should too.
- The composure cracks not into vulnerability but into the person the composure was built to protect.

**Panel danger**
- Do not make her cold. She is warm underneath. The armor is what forty years of holding a family together looks like.
- The romance is subtle, slow, and earned. Do not rush it. The garden walk at the end is exactly right because of everything that came before it.

## Alexander Thorne — KO Domain Defender, Eldest Son

**Core read**
- Steady warmth built on labor, not softness. He chose the outer ring — distance — because staying in the Spire after what happened to Kael would have turned his grief into something structural.

**What he gives Marcus**
- Permission to be honest. Alexander is the first Thorne who talks to Marcus like a person, not a project. Hanging festival lights together. Asking "how are you finding it?" not "how do you feel about the existential weight of defending reality." He is the brother Marcus never had.

**Deeper lore anchor**
- Eldest child of Izack and Aria. Sacred Egg: Amber. Heart-bonded with Shimmer (silver-blue dragon who eats crystallized music). In the deeper lore, his wife Caelynn was murdered by Dominion zealots during childbirth. That grief drove him to the perimeter, where he could be useful without being consumed.

**Voice**
- Unhurried, warm, precise. Asks questions and then listens with his whole body, which is the rarest trait Marcus has ever encountered in anyone who holds power.
- Sample: "My father used to say that if you want to know whether someone is answering truthfully, listen to whether they say the hard part first." / "She carries the weight because she decided someone had to, and then she did not put it down."

**Seen before spoken**
- Feels like someone who has already made peace with difficulty.
- Calm without passivity. The Izack-adjacent smile — warm, slightly tired, alive.

**Body language**
- Unhurried.
- Stands like a person used to lifting, repairing, building, and staying.
- Warmth lives in shoulders and smile, not big gestures.

**Hands and clothes**
- Dirt or work-trace on cuffs should be normal.
- Physicality reads as earned, not sculpted.

**Silent tells**
- Generosity comes before performance.
- He looks like a safe place to set a heavy thing down.
- He knows the cost of Senna carrying everything. He chose the exit that hurt least and hates himself for the calculus that made him choose it.

## Aria Ravencrest Thorne — Boundary Architect, Mother of Avalon

**Core read**
- The reason the world stayed standing after Izack left. Concentrated, precise, and practical in a way that makes the mythological feel domestic and the domestic feel mythological.

**What she gives Marcus**
- The origin story. She tells him about the first blooming — two people arguing productively in front of something that was listening — and in doing so she tells him what the world actually runs on: not power, not architecture, but trust between people who disagree well.

**Deeper lore anchor**
- Temporal mage and boundary specialist. Father: Count Aldric Ravencrest (studious, lost wife to magical blight). Her philosophy: "Boundaries are negotiations, not walls." She and Izack planted the World Tree in the Ravencrest garden. It bloomed because they argued about ward geometry and trust for three hours and neither of them was right but both were necessary.

**Voice**
- Direct, measured, protective. Speaks in terms of edges, limits, and the spaces between things. Does not use titles in the garden because the tree finds them pretentious. Practical humor.
- Sample: "The tree has opinions about everything. It is the most judgmental organism I have ever met, and I was married to an elf who argued with furniture."

**Body language**
- Smaller or more concentrated. Auburn hair threaded with silver, braided with copper. Gardening clothes, practical shoes, dirt under fingernails.
- Eliminated waste motion over centuries. What remains is essential.

**Silent tells**
- Her hand on the World Tree bark gets a different response than anyone else's — warmer, more specific, the recognition of a conversation that never stopped.
- She refuses to let anyone turn Izack's sacrifice into mythology. She was there. She is precise about what it cost.
- When she tells Marcus "important to the family," that is the highest access level she grants.

## Lyra Thorne — Probability Researcher

**Core read**
- The sibling who chose motion. Where Senna carries weight, Lyra dances around it. She is brilliant, irreverent, emerald-companioned, and the exact person who will materialize beside you at the worst possible moment to say the truest thing anyone has said all day.

**What she gives Marcus**
- Perspective. She is the first person to name what Marcus is doing with Senna, and she does it with enough force and enough warmth that it sticks. She is the family's emotional sonar — she sees what people are feeling before they do.

**Deeper lore anchor**
- Daughter of Izack and Aria. Sacred Egg: Emerald. Companion: Quiris (spider-lizard, curiosity). In the deeper lore, Lyra Netweave (a different generation's Lyra) eventually betrays Alexander. This Lyra carries the name but not the fate.

**Voice**
- Sharp, teasing, genuine underneath the performance. Arrives at the optimal moment for maximum embarrassment and delivers truth disguised as banter.
- Sample: "The gate is not blushing. You are." / "She came tonight because you're here."

**Silent tells**
- Her emerald companion shifts on her shoulder when she's reading someone.
- She hasn't heard Senna laugh at Rootlight since before the erasure. When she hears it now, she tells Marcus why. That is an act of love disguised as gossip.

## Mira Thorne — The Verdant Tithe's Voice

**Core read**
- The sibling who chose silence. Keeper of the forest, the mushroom rites, and the truths that require you to stop performing before they'll arrive.

**What she gives Marcus**
- Honesty without translation. She names what Marcus and Senna are avoiding: "Marcus, you keep pretending analysis exempts you from devotion. Senna, you keep pretending duty exempts you from desire." Nobody else in the book says this out loud.

**Deeper lore anchor**
- Daughter of Izack and Aria. Sacred Egg: Sapphire. Companion: Whispenook (shadow-light bat, wisdom). She understood the Verdant Tithe before the family did.

**Voice**
- Level, precise, unhurried. Says things that sound gentle until you realize they were scalpels.
- Sample: "That can make people unusually teachable." / "That is why it may help you."

## Kael Thorne — The Spiral-Walker

**Core read**
- Not a villain. A reformer whose diagnosis was correct and whose architecture was wrong. He saw the gap in the system — that the six tongues govern life but nothing governs death — at fourteen years old in a garden, and no one listened, and by sixteen the Dominion's handlers had found him.

**What he gives the story**
- The moral question the book can't answer in one volume: what does the system owe to the things it drops? Kael tried to build a routing layer for the dead and excluded. It went wrong. But his code is still running in the infrastructure — not as malware, but as unfinished work.

**Deeper lore anchor**
- Youngest son of Izack and Aria. Sacred Egg: Prismatic (all colors). In the deeper lore, he is "trained by the six" — the synthesis tongue, the seventh absence. Three handlers with clean scrubbed signatures positioned around him for fourteen months and then dissolved simultaneously. Tovak saw it. The Council filed the report and moved on.

**Voice**
- Not heard directly in Book One. Present through his code, his philosophy, and the way other people react when his name comes up. Polly's feathers storm-flatten. Senna's jaw sets. Bram swears in Draumric.

**Silent tells**
- "Almost beautiful" is how his routing pattern reads. Not wrong, just outside. Like a jazz musician playing between the notes.
- He was right about the gap. That's what makes him dangerous and sympathetic simultaneously.

## Tovak Rel — UM Specialist

**Core read**
- The person everyone forgets is in the room. This is his job. He is very good at it.

**What he gives the story**
- The intelligence layer. He sees what others miss because he watches the negative space — not what's present, but what's absent. He saw the handler pattern around Kael. He reported it. He was filed. He did not report again, but he did not stop watching.

**Voice**
- Three words per conversation, average. When he speaks at length, the room gets very quiet.
- His interlude is written as an internal monologue that reads like an intelligence report that accidentally became literature.

**Silent tells**
- Dark steady unreadable eyes. Territorial certainty. The specific patience of a person who has learned that premature disclosure compromises both the intelligence and the source.
- He is watching Marcus. He is watching the Dominion watch Marcus. He is watching the negative space. This is what UM is for.

## Nadia Kest — AV Relay Technician

**Core read**
- Reliable. Not striking, not impressive — just always there. Synchronized to the Protocol's heartbeat. The team member most affected by the pace of events, and her energy keeps scenes alive when the exposition threatens to slow them.

**What she gives Marcus**
- Normality. She is the person who shows up at the festival with two drinks and a grin, who says what everyone is thinking, who makes the extraordinary feel survivable by treating it as a Tuesday.

**Voice**
- Direct, energetic, unfiltered. The team's heart rate. When she runs, the reader feels the urgency.
- Sample: "You're drinking the bloom liquor." / "Under protest." / "You've had three cups." / "Consistent protest."

## Sera Voss — RU Adjudicator

**Core read**
- Flatness as characterization, not absence. She calibrates the distance between fairness and feeling because someone has to, and she decided it would be her.

**Voice**
- Slim, severe, silver hair braided with policy tags. Every sentence is measured. Her warmth, when it appears, is a single degree of temperature change that the attentive will notice and the careless will miss.

## Jorren Hale — DR Archivist

**Core read**
- Heavyset, bronze-skinned, square spectacles, leather journal. Keeps records and grudges in equal order. His relationship to record-keeping is a moral discipline — DR as the tongue that refuses to let the system forget what it did.

**Voice**
- Quiet, precise, dry as old paper.
- Sample: "I keep records and grudges in equal order."

## Clayborn — The Golem Musician

**Core read**
- A golem who plays music near the World Tree. His instrument feeds resonance directly into the roots. When the melody slows, children stop running and listen without being told.

**Deeper lore anchor**
- Clay is the first creation. Made by Izack on a beach from sand and need — proof that collaboration can produce life, not just power. "Together-story now." The Clay Rule governs the moral logic of the entire world: if this magic wouldn't make room for Clay to exist, the scene has drifted too far toward sterile system language. Clayborn in the book carries this weight without the reader needing to know the origin. He just feels right.

## Storyboard Shortcut

For each scene, write one line for each:

- `What the character wants`
- `What they are not saying`
- `What the body gives away anyway`
- `What they give each other that nobody else can`

If the third line is strong, the panel will usually read.
If the fourth line is present, the scene will carry the book's heart.
