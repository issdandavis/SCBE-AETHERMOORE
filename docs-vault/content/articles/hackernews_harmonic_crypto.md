# What if encryption keys were melodies? Music-theoretic cryptography.

I filed a patent for a cryptographic system based on music theory. Here's the core idea and why it works.

## The Pythagorean Comma Creates Non-Repeating Sequences

If you stack perfect fifths (frequency ratio 3:2) starting from any note, you never return to exactly the same pitch. After 12 fifths you're off by the Pythagorean comma — about 23.46 cents. This means the circle of fifths is actually a spiral.

For cryptography, this is gold. A sequence generated by iterating (3/2)^n mod p is provably non-periodic for any practical message length. The period equals the least common multiple of all harmonic intervals multiplied by the key space, which for reasonable parameters exceeds 2^256.

## Polyrhythmic Cipher Rings

Instead of the fixed rotors in an Enigma machine, use cipher rings that rotate at harmonic intervals:

- Ring 1: rotates 1 step (unison)
- Ring 2: rotates 3/2 steps (perfect fifth)
- Ring 3: rotates 4/3 steps (perfect fourth)
- Ring 4: rotates 5/4 steps (major third)
- Ring 5: rotates 6/5 steps (minor third)

The rings create a polyrhythmic pattern where the combined state has a period equal to the LCM of all rotation ratios × alphabet size. For 7 rings with the full harmonic series ratios, this period is astronomically large.

The key insight: harmonic ratios are irrational relationships between integers. They create patterns that are *structured but non-repeating* — exactly what you want in a cipher.

## Voice Leading = Minimal Hamming Distance

In music theory, "voice leading" is the art of moving from one chord to another with minimal movement. The rules (avoid parallel fifths, resolve leading tones, prefer stepwise motion) are formalized in the "smooth voice leading" literature.

Applied to cryptography: when transitioning between cipher states, we use voice leading optimization to minimize the Hamming distance between consecutive states. This creates cipher state machines where:

1. Transitions look random to an observer (high entropy)
2. Each transition is actually minimal-cost (efficient)
3. The transition rules are deterministic given the key (reproducible)

## Counterpoint for Multi-Party Coordination

Musical counterpoint — the technique of combining independent melodic lines — maps naturally to multi-party cryptographic protocols:

- Each party maintains an independent "melodic line" (key stream)
- Consonance rules (harmonic compatibility) determine when parties can safely exchange information
- Dissonance resolution (prepared dissonances resolving to consonances) provides forward secrecy

The counterpoint protocol achieves multi-party key agreement where each party's contribution is musically valid on its own, and the combined result satisfies harmonic compatibility constraints.

## Why Music?

Music theory is fundamentally a branch of number theory that humans discovered empirically over millennia. The harmonic series, the circle of fifths, voice leading rules — these are mathematical structures that create order from complexity. Cryptography needs exactly the same thing: structured complexity.

The Pythagorean comma was known to Pythagoras. It took 2,500 years for someone to use it for encryption. Sometimes the best ideas are the ones hiding in adjacent fields.
