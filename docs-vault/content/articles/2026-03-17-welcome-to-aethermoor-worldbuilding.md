# Welcome to Aethermoor: Where Languages Are Architecture

There are six sacred tongues in Aethermoor, and none of them were designed by anyone alive.

Marcus Chen was a 32-year-old software engineer working late when the transition happened. One moment he was staring at a terminal in his dim apartment, the green glow of a cursor blinking against the dark wood of his desk. The next, he was standing in a corridor made of crystal that hummed at a frequency he could feel in his teeth.

The Crystal Archive stretched above him -- shelves grown rather than built, rising thirty feet or more into a ceiling that might not have existed. Every book on every shelf glowed with its own warm light, and the combined sound was a polyphonic chord that changed as he moved through the space. The air smelled of old leather, ozone, and something he had no name for.

That was where he met Polly.

She was a raven the size of a large housecat, glossy black feathers that drank light rather than reflecting it, wearing a miniature graduation cap at a jaunty angle, a monocle over one obsidian eye, and a black silk bowtie. She looked at him the way a professor looks at a student who has wandered into the wrong lecture hall three weeks into the semester.

"You are not supposed to be here," she said. "And yet the Protocol admitted you. How uncharacteristically optimistic of it."

Polly is the Archive Keeper -- one of the oldest operational roles in Aethermoor. Her eyes are polished obsidian, smooth and reflective and unnervingly bright, and they do not change when she shifts between raven and human form. In human form she appears twenty, but her eyes carry centuries. Her hair is not hair -- it is black feathers with visible barbs and iridescent sheen. Wings fold against her back. She wears dark scholarly robes, never anything frivolous. Her transformation is, in her own words, "casual, like a window being resized."

She does not like Marcus. She also will not let him die. These are not contradictions in Aethermoor.

## The Six Sacred Tongues

Aethermoor runs on six languages, each governing a different domain of reality:

**KO** (red-gold) -- the tongue of intent and authority. The foundation. When Izack Thorne built the original architecture, KO was the first language he formalized. It handles structure, commands, and the raw grammar of how things hold together.

**AV** (blue-silver) -- the tongue of transport and routing. How information moves. The corridors of the Crystal Archive shift according to AV protocols. Doors appear when approached and vanish when passed because AV decides what connects to what.

**RU** (deep purple) -- the tongue of policy and permission. The adjudicators speak RU. When someone needs to be judged, the Oversight Chamber lights up in violet, and RU determines what is allowed.

**CA** (white-gold) -- the tongue of computation and mathematics. Bram Cortez, the maintenance lead, works in CA when he is keeping the infrastructure alive. The equations that hold Aethermoor together are written in this tongue.

**UM** (luminous shadow-black) -- the tongue of security and privacy. The darkest tongue, but not evil. UM protects. Tovak, the UM specialist, averages three words per conversation and has eyes that give nothing away.

**DR** (earth-brown, amber) -- the tongue of schema and authentication. The oldest administrative tongue. DR wristbands mark the Aether-born staff. Jorren Hale, the DR Archivist, keeps his ledger in leather and speaks in a voice so quiet you lean in without realizing it.

The tongues are not just languages -- they are physics. When the Harmonic Wall stands hundreds of feet high at Aethermoor's boundary, it is a living interference pattern of all six tongues braided together. It responds to names. It carries signatures. It flinches when it detects Kael Thorne's patches in the system.

## The Seventh Pedestal

In the Tongue Chamber, where six pedestals of different mineral each hold a Sacred Tongue sphere beneath a spiraling crystal dome, there is a seventh pedestal. It is darker, older, and bears a different script. Nobody talks about it. Polly changes the subject when Marcus asks.

The floor is hexagonal dark stone inlaid with pale crystal in a fractal pattern that repeats at every scale you look at. A 0.3-second heartbeat pulses through the room and settles in your sternum. The air tastes of ozone and dry stone.

Marcus is learning. He trusts frameworks faster than he trusts people, which in Aethermoor is both an asset and a vulnerability. His hands are always half-curled, as if he is still typing or tracing patterns in the air. He reaches for coffee that is not there.

Polly watches. She keeps her distance through precision and wit. She uses sarcasm as a shield around something older and heavier than she will admit. And when Marcus translates the world correctly -- when he sees the math beneath the magic -- something in her obsidian eyes shifts, and she is unsettled by how much it matters.
