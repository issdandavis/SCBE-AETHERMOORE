## Researched Next Steps

1. Patent Center monitoring.
   USPTO states Patent Center is used to check application status and manage
   applications. Search by application number `19/691,526` or compact number
   `19691526`.

2. OPAP/formality notices.
   USPTO explains that Office of Patent Application Processing notices identify
   what is incomplete or missing, the correction period, and any additional fees.
   If any notice appears, respond from the official notice text.

3. IDS timing.
   MPEP 609.04(b) states that, for national applications, an IDS is considered
   without fee or 37 CFR 1.97(e) statement if filed within three months of filing
   or before the first Office Action on the merits, whichever is later.

4. Office Action preparation.
   Do not amend or argue before a USPTO action requires it. Build response shells
   now so the first real Office Action can be answered from the filed claims,
   filed spec, claim-support matrix, and prior-art log.

5. New improvements.
   New technical work after 2026-05-28 should not be merged into the filed case
   record as though it were already disclosed. Track it as response evidence if
   supported by the filed spec, or as continuation/CIP candidate material.
