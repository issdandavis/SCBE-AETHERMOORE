**Field:** Acoustic cryptography and biometric authentication

### Claim 1 (Independent Method Claim)

> A method for authenticating a user within a digital environment, the method comprising:
>
> (a) generating and presenting a challenge token comprising a specific Constructed Language (Conlang) phrase to a user;
>
> (b) capturing an acoustic signal generated by the user in response to the challenge token via an acoustic sensor;
>
> (c) transforming the captured acoustic signal from a time domain to a frequency domain to generate a spectral representation;
>
> (d) extracting a harmonic fingerprint from the spectral representation, wherein the harmonic fingerprint comprises a plurality of spectral features including a ratio of odd-to-even harmonic amplitudes, a spectral centroid value, and a measure of sideband energy distribution around a fundamental frequency;
>
> (e) calculating a divergence score between the extracted harmonic fingerprint and a stored adaptive intent template associated with the user; and
>
> (f) authorizing the user only if the divergence score is below a pre-determined threshold and the spectral representation exhibits non-binary modulation characteristics defined by a minimum required spectral density between harmonic peaks.

### Claim 2 (Dependent - Dynamic Token Generation)

> The method of Claim 1, wherein the Conlang phrase is dynamically generated for each authentication session to include specific phonetic sequences selected to elicit a target fundamental frequency and a verifiable harmonic decay profile, thereby preventing replay attacks.

### Claim 3 (Dependent - Micro-Modulation Vector)

> The method of Claim 1, wherein extracting the harmonic fingerprint further comprises calculating a micro-modulation vector, said vector quantifying the rate of change of the fundamental frequency (jitter) and the rate of change of amplitude (shimmer) over the duration of the token's syllabic nucleus.

### Claim 4 (Independent System Claim)

> An authentication system comprising:
>
> an acoustic interface configured to capture an audio input;
>
> a processor coupled to the acoustic interface; and
>
> a memory storing instructions that, when executed by the processor, cause the system to:
>
> implement a Harmonic Source Enhancer to decompose the audio input into harmonic components and percussive components;
>
> execute a Spectral Comparator to compare the harmonic components against a reference Non-Binary modulation profile; and
>
> execute a Decision Engine configured to reject audio inputs classified as "Binary," wherein a Binary classification is assigned to audio inputs having spectral energy confined substantially to integer multiples of a fundamental frequency with sideband energy below a calculated noise floor.

### Claim 5 (Dependent - Rolling Key Update)

> The system of Claim 4, wherein the instructions further cause the system to update the reference Non-Binary modulation profile recursively upon each successful authentication, thereby creating a time-variant acoustic key that adapts to changes in the user's vocal characteristics over time.

### Claim 6 (Dependent - Liveness Detection)

> The system of Claim 4, wherein the decision engine is further configured to detect synthetic voice generation by analyzing the phase continuity of high-frequency harmonics, rejecting inputs that exhibit phase discontinuities characteristic of concatenated synthesis or splicing.

### Claim 7 (Dependent - Low-Power Transformation)

> The method of Claim 1, wherein the step of transforming the captured acoustic signal utilizes a Fast Fourier Transform (FFT) algorithm optimized for integer-based arithmetic on an embedded digital signal processor (DSP) to minimize energy consumption during the transformation.

### Claim 8 (Dependent - Parametric Transmission)

> The method of Claim 1, further comprising encoding the extracted harmonic fingerprint into a parametric data structure comprising frequency-amplitude pairs and envelope coefficients, and transmitting said parametric data structure over a network for remote verification, wherein the parametric data structure requires less network bandwidth than the captured acoustic signal.
