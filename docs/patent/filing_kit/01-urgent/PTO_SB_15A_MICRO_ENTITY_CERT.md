## IMPORTANT NOTES

1. **You are filing PRO SE** (without an attorney). This is completely legal for
   provisional patent applications. The USPTO allows inventors to represent
   themselves.

2. **Micro Entity saves you 80%** on the provisional filing fee. Undiscounted fee is $325.
   Small Entity is $130. Micro Entity is **$65** (base provisional filing fee).

3. **Qualification**: You qualify because you:
   - Have been named on fewer than 4 prior patent applications
   - Meet the current posted gross-income cap for micro entity ($251,190)
   - Have not assigned rights to a high-income entity

4. **If your situation changes** (income exceeds threshold, you assign to a
   company, etc.), you must notify the USPTO and pay the difference.

5. **WARNING**: False micro entity certification can result in the patent being
   held unenforceable. Only certify if ALL boxes are truthfully checked.
