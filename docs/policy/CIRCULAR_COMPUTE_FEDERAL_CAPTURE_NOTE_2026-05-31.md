# Circular Compute Federal Capture Note

Working draft: 2026-05-31

Status: SCBE-owned capture note. This is not a bid submission and does not claim eligibility, awardability, facility clearance, base access, bonding, financing, or prime-contractor readiness.

## Executive Read

The live federal market does not currently show a direct "public citizen AI compute" program. It does show two useful entry points:

1. AFRL has a live broad agency announcement for AI and next-generation distributed command and control.
2. The Air Force has a live Alaska request for lease proposal for AI data-center development on federal military installations.

Together, these validate the two strongest parts of the Circular Compute concept:

- governed distributed AI infrastructure is a real defense need;
- federal land can be used as a platform for AI data-center infrastructure.

## Policy Correction

Earlier drafts leaned too hard on CHIPS/NSTC and NAIRR as if they were already a ready hardware backbone for citizen compute.

Correct posture:

- NAIRR is a useful AI-resource coordination precedent, not a public citizen hardware utility.
- CHIPS/NSTC is useful for semiconductor workforce and research infrastructure, but reclaim labs remain a program design requirement.
- The Air Force RFLP is the strongest current precedent for using federal land to host AI data-center infrastructure.
