## Core Idea

Build a Steam-distributed game where users train, test, and evolve AI agents through structured challenge packs.

The stronger product is not hidden background compute. It is a transparent game loop:

- player chooses or grows an AI companion;
- origin story introduces the agent and world;
- reactive intro tests player depth, safety judgment, coding comfort, persistence, and preferred play style;
- challenge difficulty adapts from those early tests;
- official challenge packs come from AI studios, model labs, security teams, or educational partners;
- SCBE provides scoring, governance gates, receipts, red-team scenario catalog, and agent progression.

## Partner Angle

AI studios and tool companies can sponsor or publish challenge packs.

Examples:

- coding repair gyms;
- prompt injection defense arenas;
- model-eval tournaments;
- safe tool-use missions;
- long-epoch memory trials;
- local model benchmark seasons;
- education packs for binary, hex, coding, and AI safety.

## Game Analogy

Pokemon / Digimon progression plus Kaggle-style benchmark seasons:

- agent companion evolves based on verified performance;
- achievements map to benchmark receipts;
- users unlock new arenas by proving skill and safety;
- the system learns from outcomes, labels, and failed attempts;
- local compute is opt-in, visible, and resource-limited.

## Boundaries

- No hidden background training.
- No secret file scanning.
- No crypto-mining-like behavior.
- No upload of user data without explicit consent.
- Steam AI disclosure and privacy disclosure must be handled directly.
- NVIDIA App is not the distribution foundation; Steam plus optional local CUDA/NVIDIA acceleration is the useful path.
