# Foundation Model Composition Doctrine

## Core Principle

A model is not what it says under light. A model is what it is composed of under stress.

SCBE training should treat polished final answers as the surface of the system, not the substance of it. The durable value of a model comes from the relations stabilized during training: evidence handling, failed attempts, repair traces, verification receipts, uncertainty boundaries, and the ability to compose a reliable response under pressure.

The training target is not shine. The training target is composition.

## Doctrine

Train on becoming, not only being.

Final answers are useful, but they are incomplete training objects. A final answer shows what the model can display after the work is hidden. SCBE should prefer data that preserves the formation path:

- the first attempt,
- the failure or contradiction,
- the evidence that exposed it,
- the hypothesis or repair decision,
- the patch, revision, or rewritten answer,
- the verification result,
- the calibrated final output.

This is the difference between teaching a model to imitate confidence and teaching it to stabilize relations.

## Training Data Standard

High-value SCBE training records should include at least one of these formation traces:

- **Execution trace**: command, output, failure, fix, rerun, pass/fail receipt.
- **Reasoning repair trace**: claim, counterexample, revision, calibrated conclusion.
- **Source-evidence trace**: source lookup, extracted evidence, claim boundary, final synthesis.
- **Writing composition trace**: raw envelope, pressure map, scene draft, critique, revision.
- **Governance trace**: intent, risk surface, gate result, denial or allowed action, audit receipt.
- **Uncertainty trace**: what is known, what is inferred, what remains unverified.

Records that only contain polished answers may be kept, but they should not dominate the corpus.

## Anti-Goals

SCBE should not optimize the training corpus for rhetorical smoothness alone.

Avoid datasets that reward:

- fluent overclaiming,
- hidden failed work,
- answer-only certainty,
- pretty summaries with no receipt,
- source-free authority,
- repair-free correctness,
- safety claims without test evidence.

These traits make a model look better when prompted, but weaker when stressed.

## Domain Applications

### Coding

Preferred record:

```text
bug -> failing test/output -> diagnosis -> patch -> rerun -> verified fix
```

Less valuable record:

```text
problem -> final code
```

### Writing

Preferred record:

```text
raw image/fact/scar -> emotional pressure -> symbol choice -> scene envelope -> draft -> rhythm pass -> revision
```

Less valuable record:

```text
topic -> polished prose
```

### Research

Preferred record:

```text
question -> source search -> evidence extraction -> conflict -> claim boundary -> synthesis
```

Less valuable record:

```text
question -> confident answer
```

### Governance

Preferred record:

```text
requested action -> risk interpretation -> gate decision -> allowed/denied behavior -> audit receipt
```

Less valuable record:

```text
requested action -> policy-sounding answer
```

## Measurement

SCBE should measure trained models under pressure, not only under presentation.

Useful lift metrics include:

- recovery lift after a first failure,
- verified pass rate after repair,
- false-certification reduction,
- source-backed claim accuracy,
- refusal/allow calibration,
- audit receipt honesty,
- ability to preserve uncertainty without stalling.

Raw benchmark lift is useful, but recovery lift is often the better test of composition.

## Working Sentence

The value is in the composition of the thing, not the shine it gives when light hits it.
