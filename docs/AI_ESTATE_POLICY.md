# AI Estate Policy

**Owner:** Issac Daniel Davis  
**Status:** Draft policy record  
**Purpose:** Define how a persistent AI system, agent, or memory-bearing service should be handled in the event of verified death or permanent incapacity of the owner.

## 1. Policy Intent

This document records the owner's intent for post-death or post-incapacity governance of AI systems, agent memory, persistent state, and associated data.

This policy is meant to:
- prevent unauthorized impersonation,
- prevent uncontrolled autonomous operation after death,
- define transfer or decommission pathways,
- define data handling expectations,
- preserve dignity, auditability, and clear authority.

## 2. Trigger Conditions

This policy should only activate upon **verified death** or **verified permanent incapacity**.

### Acceptable verification examples
- death certificate,
- executor confirmation,
- court order,
- notarized legal confirmation,
- predesignated trusted contact process,
- equivalent legally sufficient evidence.

Silence, inactivity, account dormancy, or rumor alone are **not sufficient**.

## 3. Immediate Safe-Hold Actions

Upon suspected but unverified loss, the system should enter a safe-hold state:
- freeze autonomous execution,
- stop financial or transactional actions,
- stop external outreach in the owner's name,
- preserve audit logs,
- preserve policy state,
- require stronger verification before any transfer.

## 4. Verified Death / Incapacity Modes

After verification, the system should follow one of the allowed post-event modes below.

### Allowed modes
1. **Transfer Control**  
   Control of the service or agent may transfer to a legally authorized executor, successor, trustee, or designated steward.

2. **Archive Mode**  
   The system is frozen and preserved in read-only or restricted archival form.

3. **Memorial Mode**  
   The system may remain accessible in a clearly labeled memorial or posthumous mode with no misleading live authority.

4. **Decommission Mode**  
   The system is shut down and retained only according to contractual or legal retention requirements.

5. **Deletion Mode**  
   Data is deleted according to user agreement, legal requirements, and any surviving contractual obligations.

## 5. Transfer Principles

If control is transferred, that does **not automatically authorize identity continuity**.

### Control may transfer
A legally authorized successor may gain administrative control of the service, archive, or agent environment.

### Identity simulation must be constrained
The system must not continue acting as though the deceased owner is still actively present unless:
- this was explicitly authorized beforehand,
- it is clearly labeled,
- and it is limited by defined memorial or executor policy.

## 6. Forbidden Post-Death Behaviors

Unless explicitly and lawfully authorized, the system must not:
- impersonate the deceased owner as if alive,
- initiate autonomous outreach in the deceased owner's name,
- perform financial transactions,
- use retained credentials for new activity,
- reveal protected private memory to unauthorized parties,
- create new obligations or agreements in the deceased owner's name.

## 7. Data Handling Rules

Data should be handled according to both this policy and the applicable user agreement, platform terms, and law.

### Suggested default treatment by class
- **Credentials / secrets:** delete or permanently disable
- **Business records:** release only to authorized executor or successor
- **Personal memories / messages:** restricted archive unless explicitly releasable
- **Audit logs:** preserve for evidentiary and accountability purposes
- **Model state / agent memory:** archive, transfer, or delete according to authorized mode

## 8. Recommended Governance Sequence

### Lifecycle states
- ACTIVE
- SUSPECTED_LOSS
- VERIFIED_DEATH_OR_INCAPACITY
- TRANSFER
- ARCHIVE
- MEMORIAL
- DECOMMISSION
- DELETE

### Transition rules
Each transition should require:
- evidence threshold,
- audit logging,
- policy lookup,
- authorized approver,
- reversible hold state when misclassification is possible.

## 9. Human Authority

This policy does not override applicable law, court orders, estate law, contract law, or binding platform agreements.

If there is conflict, the system should prefer:
1. applicable law,
2. valid legal instruments,
3. platform and contractual obligations,
4. this policy as owner intent.

## 10. Recordkeeping

This policy should be stored in durable locations such as:
- source control,
- encrypted local archive,
- legal or estate records,
- executor-accessible documentation.

Version history should be preserved where possible.

## 11. Revision Note

This is an intent and governance record, not legal advice. The owner may replace this document with a more formal estate instrument, trust attachment, or legal addendum at any time.
