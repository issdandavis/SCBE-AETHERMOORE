# 分水宗 - Fenshui Zong - The Watershed Sect

## What It Was

The Qinling range is the watershed of the continent. Every stream on the north face runs to the Yellow River. Every stream on the south face runs to the Yangtze. For a hundred generations this was geography. The Watershed Sect made it philosophy.

They were founded on the proposition that the space between things is not empty. Between north and south, between elements, between disciplines, the divide is where something lives that neither side can see clearly from its own bank.

Their cultivation method was built around this: not mastery of a single element, but literacy in the transitions between them. The flow from one state to another. What happens at the boundary.

In practice, they produced few combat prodigies and many unusual cultivators. Mixed-root disciples who would have been assessed as middling anywhere else found the sect's methods gave them something the standard approach could not. A scattered Wind-Water root was not a liability in their system. It was a specific kind of instrument. A Wood-Metal conflict, properly worked, became a reader of both growing things and constructed ones.

They were never large. Never the most powerful. They occupied a niche the major sects tolerated because it was useful and did not threaten anyone's territory.

## Why It Fell

When the dynasty fractured, the major sects were required to declare. The Watershed Sect declined.

This was not heroism. It was consistency. A sect built around the space between things could not, without contradiction, plant its flag on one bank. They wrote letters to both factions explaining this. Neither faction appreciated the letters.

What followed was not a siege or a slaughter. The sect master, 柏云心 Bai Yun Xin, Cypress Cloud-Heart, then at Nascent Soul stage, made a calculation. The outer halls were defensible for a season, perhaps two. The inner sanctum could be sealed against anything short of a Mahayana cultivator and a great deal of time. The sect's core, its founding texts, deepest techniques, and archive of boundary-cultivation research, could be locked away intact.

She sent the disciples out in small groups with instructions to scatter, survive, and return in one generation when the war had settled. She sealed the inner sanctum herself and left through the north pass with seven senior cultivators.

They never came back.

The war did not settle so much as collapse. The dynasty fell. The people who left died, were absorbed elsewhere, forgot, or found the road home too long. The valley sat empty. The outer formation anchors cracked slowly over decades. The archive gathered dust. The inner sanctum held.

Nobody destroyed the Watershed Sect. It simply ran out of the people who knew what it was.

## What's Left

The outer halls are restored now, or mostly. The patriarch has spent 140 years doing what a formation-layer cultivator with precise Qi manipulation and limitless patience can do: reading cracked anchors, reconstructing the intent behind them, rebuilding what time took. The western wall stands. The east wing is habitable. The pill-refining chamber has three working furnace stations. The courtyard has a tree that was old when the sect was founded and did not notice the abandonment.

The archive is partially collapsed, partially legible. Sui Yue has read what he can reach. The texts are strange by standard cultivation reckoning: root types as orientations rather than ceilings, elements as directions rather than fixed identities. He does not have the framework to fully interpret what he is reading. He has been quietly certain for eight months that it matters.

The formation arrays: the patriarch has repaired most of the outer network. What he has not repaired, because he has never been sure he has the right, are the arrays directly connected to the inner sanctum seal. He understands them. He has not touched them.

The inner sanctum is intact. The patriarch's Qi sense can feel it humming beneath the courtyard stones. It is not dangerous. It is waiting. The seal Bai Yun Xin designed is not keyed to a single cultivator. It is keyed to a group: a resonance across the full range of the five elements, held simultaneously. A single powerful cultivator cannot open it. An assembled team of specialists, working the formation together, can.

The patriarch has understood this for approximately sixty years. He has been waiting for the right group to arrive.

## The Tournament

The annual competition was not invented by the village. It is a degraded form of the sect's original outer-hall admissions practice: a yearly gathering where local cultivators of any root type or background could demonstrate readiness for the sect's teaching.

The sect welcomed the mixed, the overlooked, the assessed-as-middling. The competition format was how they found them.

After the sect fell, the village kept the practice because it was what they knew and because it gave the children something to train toward. Over four generations it has become a genuine tradition: three days in the outer courtyard, sparring brackets, formation reading tests, a Qi control demonstration, and a final open challenge.

Winners receive the right to use the outer hall training space and formally study under whatever master is available, which for 140 years has meant the patriarch.

This year is different. The patriarch has repaired the last of the outer formation network. The inner sanctum arrays are connected to the activation sequence for the first time since Bai Yun Xin sealed them. He announced this quietly to no one. But the formation is live, and higher-stage cultivators can feel the shift in the valley's Qi.

Word is traveling.

The tournament this year will have visitors. Some of them will have old reasons to want what is inside.

## Spatial Storage Connection

The waystation where the patriarch found the Wuxing Chuwu Juan, the spatial storage scroll, was a Watershed Sect relay point. He did not know this when he found it. Over decades of working the scroll's technique alongside the sect archive texts, he has come to suspect it. The underlying Qi logic is too similar to be coincidence.

The spatial storage technique he has spent 140 years mastering is probably a fragment. A peripheral logistical technique, not even a core one. The full version is almost certainly in the inner sanctum.

He thinks about this more than he tells anyone.

## What The Kids Do Not Know Yet

The inner sanctum needs five elements held in resonance. The ensemble has Fire, Earth, Wind-Water, Wood-Metal, and whatever Jiang Shuang carries, which the patriarch suspects completes the sequence in a direction he has not fully mapped.

He did not assemble this group deliberately. He watched it happen and did not interfere. He is not certain whether this is wisdom or cowardice and has been considering the question for approximately two years.
