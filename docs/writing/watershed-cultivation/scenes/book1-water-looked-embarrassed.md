# Book 1 Scene - Water Looked Embarrassed

Purpose: demonstrate Yun Zhou's tendency-sense without making it a secret power reveal.

## Draft

"Yu Ping wanted to burn the doorframe."

"No," Nuan Shi said.

This had been going on for several minutes. The doorframe was rotted through and softened with winter damp, which Yu Ping said made it ideal for burning, and which Nuan Shi said made the ash load structurally unpredictable, and which Sui Yue said was beside the point because the actual problem was the drainage inscription on the lintel describing a channel running east while the water in the corridor ran west, and the obstruction must be in the secondary conduit, which-

"The water is running west," Jiang Shuang said.

"I know it's running west."

"Then why-"

"Because the inscription tells us where the block is."

Jiang Shuang looked at the water. She did not say anything else.

Da Shi was standing next to the fallen beam. It had come down sometime in autumn, maybe longer. The moss on the southeast corner suggested longer. It was the width of a man's torso and approximately the weight of a large ox. He had been ready to lift it since they arrived. He was waiting for someone to reach a conclusion.

Nuan Shi was crouched at the far wall with two fingers on a formation anchor crack, no longer participating in the doorframe argument. She had stopped participating once Yu Ping's voice climbed past a certain register.

Yun Zhou had walked past all of them and was standing in the middle of the room looking at the floor.

"The doorframe," Yu Ping said, "can be cleared in four minutes with a controlled burn-"

"You will set the sub-floor supports on fire," Nuan Shi said.

"The sub-floor is already wet."

"The sub-floor that is wet is not the sub-floor that matters. The one that matters is eighteen inches below it, and it is not wet yet."

Yu Ping closed her mouth.

Sui Yue said, "If we locate the obstruction in the eastern conduit, the water should-"

"Don't stand there."

Everyone looked at Yun Zhou.

He was looking at a point six feet in front of him, slightly left of where Sui Yue was standing.

Sui Yue looked down. The floor looked like floor.

"What?"

Yun Zhou did not answer.

Da Shi moved first. Before Yun Zhou finished speaking, before deciding to. Something in the way Yun Zhou said it. He stepped toward the wall and pulled Sui Yue with him by the sleeve.

The section of floor dropped.

Not collapsed. Dropped. A seam giving, the sub-floor settling eighteen inches into the space below. A rectangle, six feet by four, sitting down into the dark. The water in the corridor turned and began running toward it.

East.

Dust. The sound of old wood finding a new position.

Sui Yue looked at the hole where the floor had been. He looked at where he had been standing. He looked at Yun Zhou.

"Why that spot?"

Yun Zhou watched the water move.

"It looked embarrassed."

Yu Ping made a sound that was not a word.

Sui Yue opened his small book and wrote carefully: *water looked embarrassed?*

Nuan Shi had already crossed to the opening and was looking at the conduit below. Root matter, silt, a piece of old ceramic tile. She would need half an hour and a hooked tool. She said this to no one and began looking for the tool.

Jiang Shuang was still in the doorway.

She was watching Yun Zhou.

He was watching the water go the right direction. His expression was the expression of someone who had said a thing he could not explain and was hoping no one asked him to explain it.

"You are impossible," Yu Ping said.

"Yeah," Da Shi said.

* * *

The patriarch heard about it at evening. Nuan Shi told him while he was grinding compound: the corridor cleared, the sub-floor sound, the east wall anchor needing attention but not urgently, and the floor had dropped where Yun Zhou said not to stand.

The patriarch's hand kept moving.

"Dropped or collapsed?"

"Dropped. At a seam. Clean."

The hand stopped.

He was quiet long enough that Nuan Shi looked up.

"The water," he said. "What did he say about the water?"

Nuan Shi thought about it.

"He said it looked embarrassed."

The patriarch looked at his hands.

Nuan Shi waited. When it became clear he was not going to say anything else, she went back to work.

He went to the corridor after the evening meal. Stood where the floor had dropped. Looked at the seam, then the drainage line, then the lintel inscription.

Then he went to the archive room and found a text he had not opened in twenty years and read the same page three times.

He did not tell anyone what he had read.

He did not tell anyone he had gone.

## Structural Note

The scene enters on disagreement, not arrival. Each character is visible through what they are doing in the problem space. Da Shi moves before deciding because that is who he is. Jiang Shuang watches because that is who she is. Yun Zhou is right in a way he cannot explain. The patriarch's reaction is the real ending: a man says nothing, then goes alone to check a text he has not opened in twenty years.
