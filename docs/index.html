The Harmonic Wall

The core innovation: adversarial intent costs exponentially more the further it drifts from safe operation.

H(d, R) = Rd²

Where d is the hyperbolic distance from safe operation (measured in the Poincare ball) and R is the realm radius. At d=0.3, cost is ~1.1x. At d=0.95, cost explodes beyond computational feasibility.

Post-Quantum Cryptography

All cryptographic operations use NIST-approved post-quantum algorithms:

ML-KEM-768

Key Encapsulation Mechanism (formerly Kyber). Lattice-based key exchange resistant to quantum attacks.

ML-DSA-65

Digital Signature Algorithm (formerly Dilithium). Lattice-based signatures for envelope authentication.

AES-256-GCM

Symmetric encryption with authenticated additional data. Used for payload encryption inside RWP envelopes.
