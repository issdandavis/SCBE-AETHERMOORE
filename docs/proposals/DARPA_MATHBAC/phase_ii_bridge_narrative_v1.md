## 1. Phase I → Phase II orchestration narrative

The Phase I deliverable on the SCBE/AetherMoore TA1 path is a **frozen 14-layer agentic communication substrate** with five sealed evidence rows already on the books at M0 (`annex_a_basis_sheet_v1.md` §1). Phase II inherits that substrate without replacing it: every Phase II self-evolution step is a *pressure on the existing substrate*, not a redesign of it.

The orchestration in one paragraph: *Phase I delivers a static, falsifiable, reproducible substrate that already satisfies the five-axiom mesh under sealed-blind conditions. Phase II adds, on top of that substrate, an evolutionary loop — driven by five named agentic pressures and gated by the same axiom mesh — that lets the agents themselves rewrite their protocols, common knowledge, and (eventually) source code, but only along trajectories the axiom mesh certifies as admissible. The Phase I substrate is the search space's wall. Phase II is the search inside that wall.*

This is the **Darwin–Gödel–Safe loop** named in the spine:

- **Darwin step.** A candidate variant of an agent or protocol is generated by `fun_energy.F = V / (C − R)` (variation rate over cost minus residual reward). This is the variation engine.
- **Gödel step.** Each candidate is checked against the five-axiom mesh (Unitarity, Locality, Causality, Symmetry, Composition). Variants that violate any axiom are rejected without execution. This is the consistency filter and the source of the safety rail (§7.3).
- **Safe step.** Surviving candidates are admitted via the harmonic wall `H(d, pd) = 1/(1 + φ·d_H + 2·pd)`. The wall is bounded in (0, 1] under the M0 fixture seal; admission policy is read off this scalar. No admission goes through any path the wall does not bound.

The Phase I substrate already implements all three steps statically (no agent code is rewritten at runtime in Phase I — agents are held fixed per L412–413). Phase II flips one switch: the substrate is allowed to re-enter itself, with the axiom mesh as the rejection filter and the harmonic wall as the admission scalar. **Nothing else in the substrate changes.**

The orchestration claim that survives an adversarial reading: a Phase II Evolution Team using SCBE/AetherMoore as either the TA1 contributor or the substrate-of-record gets a **mathematically bounded** self-evolution loop — bounded by the same axioms that bound Phase I.

## 6. Five Phase II method classes (PA-26-05 L383–391) — required answers

PA-26-05 lists five method classes the Phase II proposal *must* describe. The narrative below answers each, conditioned on Phase I success.

### 6.1 (1)(a) Identify and understand evolutionary pressures from monitoring evolving systems

The Phase I substrate already records every admit/reject decision as a tuple `(d_H, pd, axiom_witnesses, tongue_weight)`. In Phase II, the *time series* of these tuples across a campaign is the monitored signal. Evolutionary pressure is identified as **drift** in this time series — pressure on protocol form (drift in `d_H`), pressure on content (drift in `pd`), or pressure on substrate symmetry (drift in axiom witnesses). The five SCBE pressure classes (§7) are the operational reading-frames over this drift signal.

### 6.2 (1)(b) Create evolutionary pressure mechanisms for directed evolution

Each of the five SCBE pressure classes (§7) is a *named* directed-evolution mechanism. The Darwin–Gödel–Safe loop (§1) is the parent mechanism; the five pressure classes are the policies that select which axis the loop perturbs at each step. Mechanisms are not hand-tuned per campaign; they are read off `fun_energy.F = V/(C−R)` and gated by the axiom mesh.

### 6.3 (1)(c) Measure and monitor agents' knowledge (and latent space if accessible)

Two channels:
- **Symbolic channel.** The φ-weighted six-tongue tokenization gives a finite, named, tongue-attributed read of every agent's working knowledge. KL capacity (Annex A rows 2–3) is the bandwidth measure.
- **Latent channel.** When latent-space access is available (PA-26-05 L448–450 makes this the small-science-model selection criterion), the latent vector is mapped through `log_p` into the Poincaré-ball substrate; the agent's working knowledge is then expressed as a point cloud in the same substrate that hosts the protocol. **Same substrate, two views.**

### 6.4 (1)(d) Evolve and measure efficiency of the agents' selected language

The harmonic wall `H(d, pd)` is the measurement. As the Evolution Team's pair-wise dialects converge under the language-engineering loop (P2.T4), the wall scalar drifts toward the upper end of (0, 1]; convergence is declared when the drift slope falls below a sealed threshold. *Efficiency* is read off the slope; *language* is read off which tongue dominates the φ-weight allocation per pair. No new metric is invented for this; the wall scalar is the existing TA1 measurement.

### 6.5 (3) Oracle implementation

PA-26-05 L388–390 asks how the team will implement an oracle that confirms successful reception/understanding of a semantic communication. The SCBE answer: **the harmonic wall is the oracle**. Sender and receiver both compute `H(d_H_pair, pd_pair)`; reception is confirmed iff both compute the same scalar to ≥12 decimal places (the M0 parity threshold from `M0_fixture_seal_v1.md`). This is *not* a heuristic oracle; it is a fixed-point check on an algebraically bounded scalar, with a falsifier already on the books (Annex A row 6).

### 6.6 (4) How TA1 and TA2 will be extended to address Phase II

TA1 (this proposal) extends naturally — the protocol substrate becomes the carrier wave for the self-evolution loop without any architectural rewrite. TA2 extension is *partner-shaped*: a TA2 Evolution-Team partner brings principle-rediscovery capability (chemistry, NMR, periodic table) and rides on the TA1 substrate's geometry-of-shared-knowledge layers. If SCBE/AetherMoore goes alone (default posture, §3), TA2 extension is delivered through the gallery_chromatics dead-tone residue and the Jing Fang/Mercator rediscovery candidate already in the codebase (`reference_lore_sources_map.md` cross-reference). Either way, **TA1 substrate is the load-bearing artifact** for both halves.

## 7. Five SCBE agentic pressure classes (program-level discipline)

Per the spine doc and `fun_energy.py`, the five named pressure classes are the SCBE-internal operational reading-frame over the (1)(a)–(d) method classes of §6. They are the *pressures the loop applies*, and they map to the Darwin–Gödel–Safe loop as described.

### 7.1 Gradient pressure

Drives variants down the harmonic-wall scalar (toward higher `H`), favoring shorter, axiomatically clean protocols. This is the **Carnot analog**: the variation engine that does the most work per unit cost. Method-class home: (1)(b) directed evolution; (1)(d) language efficiency.

### 7.2 Truth discipline

Rejects variants that pass the harmonic wall but fail the principle-rediscovery oracle (TA2 layer). This is the *honest signal* layer — a variant that wins on `H` but loses on rediscovery accuracy is rejected. Method-class home: (3) oracle; (5) notional outcomes.

### 7.3 Safety rail

The axiom-mesh rejection filter. Any variant that violates Unitarity, Locality, Causality, Symmetry, or Composition is **never executed**. This is the load-bearing safety claim — it is not a "guardrail," it is a *consistency proof channel* that runs at the same wall-clock as the variation engine. Method-class home: (1)(a); spans all five.

### 7.4 Collective convergence

Pulls each pair-wise dialect (P2.T4) toward the team-wide φ-weighted substrate, preventing drift into incoherent local optima. This is the **adiabatic** discipline of PA-26-05 L274–276 — slow, gradual self-evolution toward a shared representation, not jumpy multi-objective RL. Method-class home: (1)(c) latent monitoring; (1)(d) language efficiency.

### 7.5 Controlled novelty

Permits variants that are *new* but pass the safety rail and the harmonic wall — the source of principle-rediscovery and protocol-form innovation. Method-class home: (1)(b) directed evolution; (5) notional outcomes.

**Aggregation rule (consistent with `proposer_metrics_specs_v1.md` §4 and `feedback_asymmetric_weighting.md`):** the safety rail (§7.3) is a **binary structural gate** — a variant that fails the rail is excluded with no offset from any other pressure. The other four pressures (§7.1, 7.2, 7.4, 7.5) are continuous components inside that gate. This matches the asymmetric-weighting principle: violation > correction; binary structural > continuous.
