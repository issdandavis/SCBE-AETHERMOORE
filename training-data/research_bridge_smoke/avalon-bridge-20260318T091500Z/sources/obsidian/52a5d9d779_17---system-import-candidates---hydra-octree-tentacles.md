## Best Imports

### 1. Risk-tiered thresholds

From the Notion correction page:

- not one universal quorum
- threshold escalates with risk
- low-risk actions can clear at lower agreement
- high-risk actions need broader attestation

**Book translation**

- not every decision in Aethermoor should feel equally gated
- travel, archive access, council intervention, fleet deployment, and death-ledger revision should all feel like different threshold classes
- Marcus can notice that the system is more mature than a binary allow/deny model

**Best use**

- Dremsreach
- council conflict
- post-Shardfold doctrine revision

### 2. Ordered attestation paths

From the same Notion page:

- sequence matters
- first signer establishes context
- later signers attest prior validity
- chain: `KO -> AV -> RU -> CA -> UM -> DR`
- many possible ordered paths exist

**Book translation**

- witness order matters
- the same six tongues do not always speak in the same order
- political and ritual weight can change depending on who attests first

**Best use**

- formal rites
- council scenes
- Ame-related validation callbacks
- any scene where Marcus realizes governance is choreography, not just permission

### 3. Attestation-path diversity, not fake security bragging

From the Notion correction page:

- do **not** overclaim a fake cryptographic multiplier
- what actually matters is path diversity, replay resistance, and workflow richness

**Book translation**

- the world should feel robust because it has many truthful ways to verify something
- not because someone says it is mathematically invincible
