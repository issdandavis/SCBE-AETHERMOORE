# The Six Tongues Protocol - Everweave Timeline

Compressed story spine from the Everweave campaign material.

Use this as deep history and thematic scaffolding, not as a demand to rewrite the isekai into the old campaign.

## Core Reading Rule

- The isekai is still Marcus's book.
- Everweave supplies the older emotional architecture of the world.
- Use it to sharpen history, interludes, family resonance, and worldview.

## Timeline Spine

1. Izack wakes with no memory on impossible shores near the wreck of the Threshold Seeker.
- Emptiness is the first wound.
- Curiosity is the first survival response.

2. Izack follows traces inland and begins reading the world as a set of living magical structures.
- This is the earliest form of the later systems / protocol logic.

3. Izack encounters Eldrin in the forest.
- The alliance begins through mapping, study, and practical problem solving.

4. Polly enters early as interpreter, witness, and sharp-tongued guide.
- The relationship begins as conversation, not obedience.

5. The cave / living rune sequence becomes the first major breakthrough.
- Magic is language.
- Boundaries are active.
- Meaning alters outcome.

6. Clay emerges as the first unconscious pact.
- Need creates relation before theory catches up.
- This is one of the deepest canon locks in the whole setting.

7. Izack begins shaping inner-space / storage / dimensional refuge into the seed of Avalon.
- He responds to uncertainty by building habitable structure.

8. Aria enters as counterpart in both philosophy and love.
- Boundary magic is not anti-connection.
- It creates safe conditions for connection.

9. Avalon expands as living home, not conquered empire.
- food
- ecology
- architecture
- roads
- rooms
- students
- domestic systems

10. Zara arrives and becomes part student, part family, part future-force.
- Izack's version of power keeps expanding into care.

11. The gazebo sequence happens early in the woods / cave exploration period.
- Izack gets bored of walking and builds sanctuary.
- It becomes part shrine, part research station, part rest point.
- This is pure character truth: under pressure, he makes shelter.

12. The gazebo later evolves into a more advanced portable or linked structure tied back to Avalon.
- Sanctuary becomes infrastructure.

13. The Verdant Forest / mushroom spore sequence carries memory, altered perception, and origin-pressure.
- This is a strong place for truth, ancestry, and destabilized self-knowledge.

14. The late divine scale-expansion beat is Ame.
- By this stage the book-world has already proven Izack's values.
- Divine contact expands scale; it does not replace the emotional thesis.

15. The end-state is not imperial victory.
- It is tea, cake, children, family, and large shared outings.
- The final truth is domestic and relational.

## What To Carry Into The Isekai

- Clay as moral logic
- Polly as recontextualizer
- boundary + invitation as compatible magic philosophies
- sanctuary-building as Izack's signature impulse
- family warmth after cosmic scale

## What Not To Do

- Do not replace Marcus's POV journey with old campaign summary.
- Do not lore-dump the whole Everweave chronology into the main narrative.
- Do not make every historical echo explicit.

## Best Uses

- interludes
- background myths
- council remarks
- archive fragments
- family references
- subtle emotional echoes in sacred-tongue scenes
