## The Seven Senses

1. Sight
- Crystal growth, rune-lattice, refracted light, feather sheen, protocol geometry.

2. Sound
- Hums, pulses, harmonics, feather-clicks, archive resonance, tongue-counterpoint.

3. Smell
- Ozone, old paper, hot metal, tea, wet moss, berry sugar, rain on crystal.

4. Taste
- Stale coffee, static on the tongue, blood-metal panic, mineral air, healing fruit.

5. Touch
- Cold stone, warm crystal, pressure shifts, boundary resistance, feather drag, dry paper.

6. Time
- Protocol heartbeat, delay, temporal drag, forest rhythm, stretched perception, cave-time.

7. Magic / Cryptography
- Wards as policy.
- Keys as trust.
- Signatures as identity.
- Binding as authenticated relationship.
- Echoes as distributed nodes.
- Corruption as forged legitimacy.

## Usage Rule

- In most scenes, use 2 physical senses and 1 extra sense.
- The extra sense is usually Time or Magic/Cryptography.
- Save full sensory overload for breakthrough, panic, or revelation scenes.

## Marcus Translation Layer

Use lightly.
One line is usually enough.

- ward -> firewall with policy enforcement
- soul-signature -> cryptographic identity
- binding -> trusted auth relationship
- sacred tongue -> constrained execution language
- archive verification -> integrity check
- corruption -> forged or poisoned state
- ritual circle -> execution environment
- harmony -> consensus
- Echoes -> distributed nodes

Marcus should not always be right.
He should often grasp the structure and miss part of the human meaning.

## Clay Rule

Clay must quietly govern the moral logic of the world even when absent.

- first unconscious pact
