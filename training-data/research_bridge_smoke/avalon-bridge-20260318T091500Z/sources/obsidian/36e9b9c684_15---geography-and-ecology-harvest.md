## Safe Backbone

### Aethermoor

- broad civilizational frame with named peoples:
  - `Sylvani`
  - `Drakmori`
  - `Nordgrim`
  - `Sunward Folk`
  - `Deepstone Folk`
- institutional texture:
  - `Grand Academy`
  - `Order of Silver Flame`
  - `Circle of Speakers`

Use this as distant-scale history and species/culture pressure, not immediate exposition.

### Avalon / Pollyoneth

- a living dimensional heart built on spiraling leylines
- recurring environmental features:
  - `floating isles`
  - `time-controlled zones`
  - `Dream Gardens`
  - `Leyline Gazebo`
  - `Spiral Spire`
- the realm behaves like a partner:
  - paths form themselves along optimal ley routes
  - rooms and infrastructure respond to inhabitant needs
  - architecture feels grown, not built

Strong atmospheric pull from the exports:

- buildings rise like `architectural flowers`
- paths are carved along ley lines for foot traffic
- the realm is old-feeling the moment it exists
- the World Tree acts as heart, anchor, and memory-root

## Ravencrest Geography / Threshold Texture

The strongest Ravencrest export material is not just political; it is spatial.

Recurring traits:

- manor exists in more dimensions than visitors are allowed to see
- the estate is the product of `centuries of careful cultivation`
- every path curves to invite exploration
- every stone is placed with intention
- every magical resonance is tuned, not loud

This is ideal for the isekai because it lets Marcus read:

- threshold architecture
- permissions embedded in space
- trust expressed as what the house reveals

## Ecology / Bestiary / Living Systems

### World Trees and Root Ecology

- World Trees do not just grow; they plan
- roots anchor realms and can thread dimensions
- root systems can become visible energy networks
- root tips are unstable thresholds in some export branches

Use for:

- routing, anchoring, homing, naming, memory
- public rituals about names being carried correctly

### Dryads / Gardening Orders

- `dryad enclaves` are directly named for the Verdant Tithe
- other export lanes mention `dryads and magicians tending` the Tree
- `gardening nymphs` appear in the chat export

Use for:

- forest keepers
- horticultural magic workers
- emotional and ecological auditors

### Seafolk / Shardfold Populations

- `half-seafolk` is the clean export shorthand

Use for:

- amphibious trade culture
- crystal/tide burial customs
- reef memory and tide law

### Deepstone / Mountain Folk

- `Deepstone Folk` are named in the Aethermoor summary file
- mountain / forge / runic-engineering flavor is repeatedly associated with dwarven and stone cultures

Use for:

- Irondeep adjacency
- machinery plus runic craft
- pressure, heat, precision, stubborn civic pride

### Mounts / Constructs / Living Utility

- `Stormwind` = magically bonded horse, emotional mount
- `Clayborn` = music-sensitive golem / construct learning soul
- early exports repeatedly treat constructs as evolving toward witness/personhood rather than staying equipment

Use for:

- utility creatures that have interiority
- bonded mounts that know weather, fear, or paradox before riders do
- public performances or labor roles for constructs that express personhood

### Flora

Export texture strongly supports:

- mystic orchards
- Dream Gardens
- luminescent crops responsive to intention
- flowers that choose to bloom in response to emotional/magical shifts
- gardens as threshold technologies, not decoration

Use for:

- festivals
- courtship/history scenes
- proving a place is alive through horticulture

## Travel Infrastructure

Good recurring infrastructure motifs from the exports:

- leyline roads
- dimensional folds as travel corridors
- rootways / root stairs
- portals that need trust / recognition
- roads that know names or current voices
- warded estates and region-scale boundary logic

This is important for the isekai because Marcus should not just move through empty map-space.
He should feel the world as:

- routed
- permissioned
- cultivated
- historically layered

## Festival / Public-Life Hooks

The exports do not hand over one definitive festival package, but they do support:

- orchards of mystic plants
- lanterns / held lights
- feasts from magical agriculture
- root-bound public spaces
- Ravencrest formal hospitality
- World Tree blooming as family history and public myth

This is enough to keep `Rootlight` feeling native instead of invented.

- Do not import every old region note as hard canon automatically.
- Use the export material as texture first, title map second.
- If a live manuscript chapter contradicts an old lore file, the live manuscript wins.
- Prefer details that improve scenes:
  - smell
  - weather
  - labor
  - architecture
  - ritual
  - creature behavior
