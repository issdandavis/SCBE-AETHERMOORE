## Visual Presence Rule

Do not let characters exist only as voices, titles, or system-functions.

In new scenes, especially introductions, returns, festivals, councils, travel chapters, and battle staging, give readers:

- one immediate visual lock
- one texture detail
- one movement or posture detail

Good anchor types:

- face
  - crow's-feet
  - sleepless shadows
  - freckles
  - smile lines
  - weathered skin
- hair
  - braid logic
  - curl pattern
  - frizz from forge heat
  - rain-flattened wave
  - orchard pollen in dark hair
- body / motion
  - jaw set
  - one-corner smile
  - hand habit
  - shoulder set
  - how they cross a room

Use physical detail for three jobs:

1. `First picture`
   - the reader should be able to see someone quickly.
2. `Emotional leakage`
   - stress, warmth, grief, attraction, fatigue, and control should show up in the body.
3. `Continuity`
   - recurring small details make later scenes land faster.

Avoid full inventory paragraphs unless the moment is built for it. Seed, then deepen later.
