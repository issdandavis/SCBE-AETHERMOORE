# Personality Rubix

Writer-facing character matrix for `The Six Tongues Protocol` and the supporting Avalon family lore.

This is not a diagnostic document. It is a drafting tool.

## Model Stack

### Sanderson Lenses

- `Proactivity` — do they drive events or mostly react?
- `Relatability` — how easily does a reader emotionally connect to them?
- `Capability` — how effective are they in their domain?

Scale: `1-5`

### Psychology Lenses

- `Big Five shorthand`
  - `O` openness
  - `C` conscientiousness
  - `E` extraversion
  - `A` agreeableness
  - `N` neuroticism / emotional volatility
- `Attachment stance`
  - secure
  - anxious / preoccupied
  - dismissive / avoidant
  - fearful / disorganized

These are writer shorthand, not clinical labels.

### Story / Lore Lenses

- `Core wound`
- `Surface want`
- `Deep need`
- `Public mask`
- `Stress pattern`
- `Humor mode`
- `System role`
- `Promise -> Pressure -> Payoff`

## Visual Anchors

Use these as recurring picture-memory cues. Do not dump them all at once. Seed one or two per scene.

- `Marcus`
  - dark hair that only behaves when ignored
  - tired eyes with screen-life shadows still in them
  - face sharpens when he is thinking and softens late, after he has already felt something
- `Polly`
  - obsidian-black feather sheen shifting violet / oil / night-sky in good light
  - old bright eyes that make amusement and judgment arrive together
  - in humanoid form, movements too precise to ever feel fully ordinary
- `Senna`
  - composed face with fatigue stored at the corners of the eyes, not the mouth
  - dark hair controlled with practical severity
  - posture so exact it reads as architecture before it reads as beauty
- `Lyra`
  - Izack-dark hair that never quite stays where it was put
  - quick grin, quick hands, kinetic face
  - looks like motion even when standing still
- `Mira`
  - darker, quieter presence; face often half-hidden by angle, book, branch, or shadow
  - soft still eyes that miss very little
  - carries retreat as a visual boundary, not a weakness
- `Alexander`
  - open face with early laugh-lines and eldest-child steadiness
  - dirt on cuffs or practical wear looks right on him
  - calm shoulders, deliberate hands, one-corner approval smile
- `Kael`
  - watchful eyes that arrive before the rest of the expression
  - thin, quick body-language; space seems negotiable around him
  - smile often lands half a beat before trust should
- `Izack`
  - dark hair gone slightly unruly under pressure and thought
  - tired kind eyes with too-late-dinner humanity still visible in them
  - robes / sleeves / cuffs usually show he was building something five minutes ago
- `Aria`
  - auburn hair threaded with copper or silver depending on era
  - one eyebrow does half her criticism
  - practical grace; even garden clothes look load-bearing
- `Clayborn`
  - shaped for gentleness rather than intimidation
  - polished surfaces warmed by use, music, or care
  - pauses like someone listening for the room's safety before speaking
- `Stormwind`
  - mane alive with static-rune weather
  - calm eyes that refine under pressure rather than widen
  - movement feels like route certainty given muscle
- `Bram`
  - built like maintenance with bad sleep and stubborn loyalty
  - hands that look capable of breaking things but usually fix them instead
  - face settles into suspicion before affection, but affection is there
- `Nadia`
  - wind-burned energy, grin first, caution later
  - hair and stance rarely fully still
  - reads like a route already in motion
- `Jorren`
  - still, square, tidy, impossible to surprise into visible disorder
  - spectacles / slate / ink details matter more than wardrobe flourish
  - face gives away amusement only under protest
- `Sera`
  - severe beauty built from precision, not softness
  - silver hair braided with policy tags / controlled detail
  - face almost never overreacts, which makes small changes carry force
- `Tovak`
  - shadowed restraint more than ornament
  - expression economy so severe that attention itself feels like impact
  - held violence, never wasted
- `Eldrin`
  - elder polish with threshold humor in the eyes
  - age visible as elegance, not frailty
  - carries ceremony as if it were practical engineering
- `Zara`
  - heat-marked intensity; fire belongs in the silhouette before the spell
  - hair and posture both suggest contained combustion
  - strong features sharpen further when challenged
- `Malzeth'irun`
  - dangerous stillness under careful discipline
  - body language reads compressed, not relaxed
  - visible strain should feel like control under pressure, not simple menace
- `Ame`
  - too exact to read as merely beautiful
  - script-light / geometry / authority visible before personality
  - feels illuminated from principle rather than light source

## Core Cast

### Marcus Chen

- `Core wound`: displacement, unbelonging, and the fear that if he cannot understand the system he will never truly live inside it.
- `Surface want`: survive, decode, become useful.
- `Deep need`: accept that belonging is relational, not purely intellectual.
- `Public mask`: dry competence, skeptical systems-thinker, deadpan.
- `Stress pattern`: overmodels, narrows emotionally, tries to solve people like routing problems.
- `Humor mode`: deadpan technical understatement.
- `System role`: outsider auditor who becomes bridge architecture.
- `Promise -> Pressure -> Payoff`: outsider engineer reads magic as system -> world keeps proving it is more relational than code -> Marcus discovers both views are true together.

### Polly

- `Core wound`: she has remembered too many endings.
- `Surface want`: keep the world alive and stop idiots from mistaking domination for intelligence.
- `Deep need`: let herself invest in new people without treating hope as procedural risk.
- `Public mask`: sarcasm, theatrical omniscience, academic aggression.
- `Stress pattern`: precision sharpens into cruelty if grief is hit directly.
- `Humor mode`: punctures pomp, weaponized commentary, affectionate mockery.
- `System role`: witness, archivist, and translator between literacies.
- `Promise -> Pressure -> Payoff`: impossible raven mentor -> old grief resurfaces through Marcus/Kael -> becomes the keeper who can still choose attachment.

### Senna Thorne

- `Core wound`: Kael, continuity burden, and years of carrying what institutions would not say aloud.
- `Surface want`: keep everything from breaking.
- `Deep need`: let care be shared; stop confusing sole responsibility with love.
- `Public mask`: composure, precision, administrative stillness.
- `Stress pattern`: compresses affect, becomes more exacting, overfunctions.
- `Humor mode`: dry fatalism and surgical timing.
- `System role`: operational continuity; the person who keeps the living structure from collapsing.
- `Promise -> Pressure -> Payoff`: severe continuity chair -> Marcus and Kael reopen the fracture -> she learns continuity can include vulnerability, not just endurance.

### Lyra Thorne

- `Core wound`: being mistaken for volatility instead of intelligence; being read as noise when she is really variance with pattern.
- `Surface want`: movement, experimentation, more world.
- `Deep need`: respect limits without feeling caged by them.
- `Public mask`: speed, wit, shamelessness.
- `Stress pattern`: improvises harder, jokes louder, resists stillness.
- `Humor mode`: sibling callouts, delight in exposing hypocrisy.
- `System role`: probability, mobility, destabilizing stale assumptions.
- `Promise -> Pressure -> Payoff`: kinetic twin -> variance becomes strategic truth -> learns how to turn speed into durable stewardship without losing spark.

### Mira Thorne

- `Core wound`: retreat mistaken for absence; sensitivity mistaken for fragility.
- `Surface want`: solitude, clarity, selective contact.
- `Deep need`: let trusted people cross the threshold into her interior world.
- `Public mask`: quietness, scholarship, distance.
- `Stress pattern`: withdraws, withholds, tries to metabolize danger alone.
- `Humor mode`: rare precision-strikes; dry lines that land harder because they are rare.
- `System role`: memory, forest logic, emotional cost accounting.
- `Promise -> Pressure -> Payoff`: hidden twin / interior intelligence -> world encroaches through memory hunger -> becomes the character who teaches that stillness can also be active defense.

### Alexander Rune Thorne

- `Core wound`: heir-pressure and being treated as destiny before he has finished becoming a person.
- `Surface want`: help everyone collaborate better.
- `Deep need`: accept that not every future can be carried by kindness alone.
- `Public mask`: gentle confidence, big-brother steadiness.
- `Stress pattern`: takes on too much responsibility gracefully, which makes it harder for others to notice.
- `Humor mode`: earnest literalism and good-faith questioning.
- `System role`: collaborative heir, synthesis leader, proof that inheritance can be generous.
- `Promise -> Pressure -> Payoff`: gifted heir of collaborative magic -> burden and scale intensify -> becomes a leader whose power is still hospitality.
- `Marcus interaction key`: he is the first sibling most likely to give Marcus unpressured space; side-by-side tasks work better than heart-to-heart talks.

### Kael Nightwhisper Thorne

- `Core wound`: sees systems at a level that isolates him; brilliance curdles into estrangement. Compounded by external grooming that validated his isolation and aimed it at load-bearing architecture.
- `Surface want`: correct the system, prove the diagnosis, stop failure at the root.
- `Deep need`: accept that collaboration cannot be safely replaced by his own superior architecture. Accept that the friends who understood him best were handlers, not peers.
- `Public mask`: mischief, brilliance, composure under impossible complexity.
- `Stress pattern`: isolates, centralizes, justifies control because he can see the flaw.
- `Humor mode`: impossible literalism, elegant troublemaking, spatial pranks.
- `System role`: genius fracture; the temptation to solve trust with authority. His hospice for the dead was the system's first self-improvement cycle — the idea was right, the method was catastrophic.
- `Promise -> Pressure -> Payoff`: brilliant youngest son -> diagnosis is right but treatment becomes catastrophic -> planted handlers aimed his brilliance at centralization -> either tragedy or difficult reintegration, depending on endgame.
- `Key detail`: his language shifted from "fixing the system" to "replacing the architecture." Fixing is what engineers do. Replacing is what conquerors do.
- `Dominion connection`: handlers arrived over 2 years, vanished when consequences landed. Pattern, not proof. Rebuilding governance = dissolving distributed keys = Dominion access.

## Foundational Lore Figures

### Izack Elion Thorne

- `Core wound`: memory-loss emptiness and the original loneliness beneath all his building. Now compounded by binding himself into the architecture to prevent collapse — present but not embodied, unable to reach his own son.
- `Surface want`: understand magic, build home, love his people well.
- `Deep need`: accept that invitation needs structure and boundaries to endure. Accept that holding everything together is not the same thing as being available.
- `Public mask`: absent-minded wonder, poetic engineer, hopeful architect. In current state: the system's voice, its pulse, its opinion about your calibration.
- `Stress pattern`: researches instead of resting, builds instead of grieving. Has been holding the space for the seventh silence manually for decades. That's why he's tired.
- `Humor mode`: philosophical nerding, accidental comedy. Still has opinions about your calibration. Still corrected by Polly. Still smokes Fizzlecress ("Frizka'leth," Polly corrects).
- `System role`: founder of collaborative dimensional architecture. Currently: load-bearing substrate. Exists in full 21D canonical state.
- `Promise -> Pressure -> Payoff`: scholar washed into impossible world -> builds family and realm through invitation -> binds himself into the architecture to hold it together when his son nearly breaks it -> asks Marcus to bring Kael home.
- `Key scene`: crystal observatory, Fizzlecress pipe, tired kind eyes, still a person. When Marcus meets him: not a ghost, not a god — a father who became the building because no one else could hold the weight.
- `Everweave anchors`: beach awakening, Polaris wreck, Ravencrest sigil, forest pulse investigation, Polly bond, Aria reunion at Silvermist, Avalon dimensional creation, Ame pact, crystal helix chamber over 47 realities.

### Aria Ravencrest Thorne

- `Core wound`: responsibility to threshold, family, and order in a world that can rupture.
- `Surface want`: protect what matters.
- `Deep need`: let tenderness stay visible; not everything must be earned through utility.
- `Public mask`: restraint, precision, authority.
- `Stress pattern`: controls environment, narrows choices, becomes all edge.
- `Humor mode`: one-line surgical corrections.
- `System role`: boundary architect; shape, law, and safety.
- `Promise -> Pressure -> Payoff`: disciplined protector -> love and family complicate control -> becomes the proof that structure can create intimacy instead of choking it.

### Clayborn

- `Core wound`: being made by need and always risking reduction to function.
- `Surface want`: learn, help, become more fully a person.
- `Deep need`: own agency beyond being useful to the person who first called him.
- `Public mask`: gentle steadiness.
- `Stress pattern`: seeks resonance; can overidentify with others' needs.
- `Humor mode`: literal innocence and unexpected depth.
- `System role`: first unconscious pact; personhood through welcome.
- `Promise -> Pressure -> Payoff`: helper construct -> becomes real person through music, care, and time -> proves relationship precedes architecture.

### Eldrin Ravencrest

- `Core wound`: the elder burden of watching thresholds hold and generations turn.
- `Surface want`: cultivate good inheritors.
- `Deep need`: let descendants fail in their own ways instead of over-curating them.
- `Public mask`: wit, ceremony, dry patience.
- `Stress pattern`: becomes more formal and more ancient.
- `Humor mode`: dry threshold humor and aristocratic understatement.
- `System role`: mentor, gatekeeper, elder theorist of boundaries.
- `Promise -> Pressure -> Payoff`: threshold elder -> family and realm destabilize -> remains the man who can teach manners to bridges and people alike.

### Zara

- `Core wound`: survival, exclusion, and needing to prove she belongs.
- `Surface want`: master her power and make it useful.
- `Deep need`: stop equating worth with performance.
- `Public mask`: intensity, invention, fire.
- `Stress pattern`: escalates force and speed.
- `Humor mode`: bluntness, flame-metaphor honesty.
- `System role`: dragonkin engineer, catalytic chosen-family member.
- `Promise -> Pressure -> Payoff`: damaged brilliant apprentice -> becomes maker/teacher -> learns family is not conditional.

### Malzeth'irun

- `Core wound`: being treated as political risk, inherited danger, and category problem before person.
- `Surface want`: learn control without becoming smaller than she is.
- `Deep need`: trust that she can belong without falsifying any part of her inheritance.
- `Public mask`: restraint, courtesy, carefully rationed force.
- `Stress pattern`: compresses harder, overcontrols, expects rejection before it arrives.
- `Humor mode`: dry candor from someone who learned very early that sugar-coating alarm only wastes time.
- `System role`: integration test for the Protocol; difference held without collapse.
- `Promise -> Pressure -> Payoff`: dangerous child / demon-line edge case -> becomes bridge character through disciplined integration -> proves threat-signature and moral failure are not the same thing.

## Spire Cast

### Bram

- `Core wound`: care buried under labor.
- `Surface want`: keep the machinery running.
- `Deep need`: admit maintenance is intimacy and not just duty.
- `Public mask`: gruffness, anti-romantic practicality.
- `Stress pattern`: reaches for tools before language.
- `Humor mode`: terrible coffee and blunt refusals.
- `System role`: builder / maintainer / reality through hardware.
- `Promise -> Pressure -> Payoff`: gruff engineer -> becomes one of Marcus's anchors -> proves competence can be warmth in work clothes.

### Nadia

- `Core wound`: motion used to outrun fear.
- `Surface want`: act fast, hit true, stay useful.
- `Deep need`: trust that stillness and tenderness are not weakness.
- `Public mask`: swagger, speed, irreverence.
- `Stress pattern`: gets louder, faster, more kinetic.
- `Humor mode`: affectionate menace and momentum.
- `System role`: AV courage, morale, movement.
- `Promise -> Pressure -> Payoff`: kinetic operator -> learns when speed helps and when witness matters more -> remains joy under pressure.

### Jorren

- `Core wound`: distrust of entropy, ambiguity, and undocumented damage.
- `Surface want`: correct record, stable proof.
- `Deep need`: remember people are not documentation errors.
- `Public mask`: paperwork, exactness, administrative serenity.
- `Stress pattern`: doubles down on categorization.
- `Humor mode`: timestamp as insult.
- `System role`: DR record-keeper and proof spine.
- `Promise -> Pressure -> Payoff`: dry archivist -> becomes unexpectedly load-bearing emotionally -> proves record can be care.

### Sera

- `Core wound`: institutional fragility; she has seen what policy failures cost.
- `Surface want`: make governance strong enough to survive human error.
- `Deep need`: remember policy exists to protect people, not reverse the relationship.
- `Public mask`: authority, compression, exacting standards.
- `Stress pattern`: formalizes harder.
- `Humor mode`: accidental bureaucratic deadpan.
- `System role`: RU spine; law, process, legitimacy.
- `Promise -> Pressure -> Payoff`: severe authority -> learns to trust new data and new people -> remains hard, but not inert.

### Tovak

- `Core wound`: proximity to shadow and long containment work.
- `Surface want`: know the truth before sentiment contaminates it.
- `Deep need`: secrecy and solitude are not sufficient moral structures.
- `Public mask`: silence, shadow, operational calm.
- `Stress pattern`: vanishes into execution.
- `Humor mode`: almost none; accidental void deadpan.
- `System role`: UM witness, containment specialist.
- `Promise -> Pressure -> Payoff`: shadow operator -> becomes one of the clearest truth-tellers in the system -> proves care can look like severity.

## Principle Figure

### Ame

- `Core function`: wisdom witnessed in full light. Goddess of Wisdom, Accumulated Knowledge, and Occasional Guidance.
- `Do not write as`: random god power, easy plot button, generic benevolent goddess. Not omnipotent but collaborative.
- `Write as`: attestation, lawful recognition, root-of-trust, authority without domination. Requires pacts to manifest power.
- `Relational mode`: she validates burdens already earned; she does not remove them. Her wisdom is "occasional" — probabilistic, not guaranteed.
- `System role`: divine attestation layer.
- `Promise -> Pressure -> Payoff`: distant wisdom-presence -> appears only when enough has been carried -> legitimizes stewardship rather than solving it.
- `Everweave anchors`: responded to Izack's forest prayers, formed pact during subspace creation, blessed Chronological Nexus Staff with temporal navigation, created Crystal Garden, planted divine seeds. Manifests as luminous raven or shimmering veil (blends with Polly's nature). Her pact is mutual — she gains a mortal anchor, implying gods need collaboration too.

## Use Rules

- Do not dump this onto the page.
- Use it to choose reactions, silence, and contradiction.
- If a scene feels flat, check whether the character is acting from `mask` instead of `wound/want`.
- If a subplot feels fake, check whether the `promise -> pressure -> payoff` line is actually being honored.
- If two characters sound the same, compare their `humor mode` and `stress pattern`.
