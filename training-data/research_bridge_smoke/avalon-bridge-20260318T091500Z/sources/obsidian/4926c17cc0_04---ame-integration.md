## Important Constraint

Ame in Everweave reads as `wisdom / knowledge`, not brute command-authority.

For the isekai, the strongest translation is:

- `pure authority through wisdom`
- `legitimate attestation`
- `root-of-trust recognition`

Not:
- dictator-god
- replacement for the six-tongue distributed model
- active centralized ruler

If she becomes simple command-authority, she starts overlapping with the Void Seed problem and weakens the book's argument.

## Everweave Source Details

From the original 528-page Everweave campaign log:
- Ame manifests around pages 300-400 in response to Izack's accumulated quest for wisdom
- Pact formed during subspace ecosystem creation (pages 400-500)
- She is not omnipotent but collaborative — requires pacts to manifest power
- Creates / anchors the Crystal Garden of Ame
- Blesses Izack's Chronological Nexus Staff for temporal navigation
- Divine seeds planted in temple planning sequence (pages 520-528)
- Manifests as luminous raven or shimmering veil (blends with Polly's polydimensional nature)

Key Ame attributes from the bible:
- **Wisdom and insight**: grants clairvoyant pulses, multi-timeline glimpses
- **Accumulated knowledge**: runic blessings granting access to forgotten lore
- **Guidance through pacts**: contractual bonds providing boons — mutual, not one-directional
- **Non-interventionist**: avoids direct combat, focuses on enlightenment
- **Occasional**: her guidance is probabilistic — can fail if the pact-holder lacks vulnerability

The pact is mutual — Ame gains a mortal anchor, implying gods need collaboration. This ties directly to the book's argument: collaboration scales better than domination.
