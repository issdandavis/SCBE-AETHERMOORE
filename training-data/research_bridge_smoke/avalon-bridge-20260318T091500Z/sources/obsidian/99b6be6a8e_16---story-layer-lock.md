# Story Layer Lock

This is the current governing model for `The Six Tongues Protocol`.

The book is already good.
The job now is not to reinvent it.
The job is to deepen it without breaking its spine.

## The Three-Layer Story

### Layer 1: Human Story

This is the part readers live in first.

- found family
- friendship
- attraction / slow-burn relationship tension
- battle / travel / adventure
- belonging vs. exile

If the book fails here, none of the other layers matter.

Marcus has to feel:

- lonely
- useful
- increasingly attached
- torn between leaving and staying
- surprised by how much this world starts to matter to him

The Thorne family, the Spire crew, and the wider world need to feel like people, not lore terminals.

### Layer 2: Magical / Philosophical Story

This is the world’s own truth about itself.

- magic as collaboration
- invitation over domination
- governance as relationship
- names, witness, memory, and continuity
- families and realms as living systems with emotional cost

This is where Izack, Aria, Polly, Clay, the World Tree, and the sibling geometry matter most.

The world does **not** think of itself as software.
It thinks of itself as:

- magic
- thresholds
- inheritance
- ritual
- work
- grief
- beauty

### Layer 3: Marcus's Programmer Lens

This is interpretation, not replacement.

Marcus sees:

- leylines as something like routing
- wards as something like firewalls
- names as identity and trust primitives
- sacred tongues as constrained execution languages
- continuity as system resilience
- corruption as forgery / adversarial intrusion

This layer should make the book sharper, not flatter.

Marcus is not here to explain away wonder.
He is here to notice structure inside wonder.

## Core Rule

The world is magic-first.
Marcus is systems-literate.
Both are true at the same time.

## Architectural Stakes

The six-tongue architecture is not just governance.
It is also the lock.

- the keys are not objects
- the keys are the relationships between the tongues
- distributed verification is what keeps the outer threat out
- centralization is not merely a political style difference
- centralization is a breach condition

This means:

- the Dominion does not need to steal a single literal master key
- the Dominion needs the system to believe unity is safer than consensus
- Kael's old critique becomes dangerous precisely because parts of it are true

## Kael Rule

Kael is not strongest when written as:

- lone mad genius
- secret monster
- internal rot made flesh

Kael is strongest when written as:

- brilliant
- sincere
- legitimately frustrated by real system failures
- increasingly persuaded that distributed governance is weakness
- manipulated by outside actors who recognized his grievance as an access path

So the working model is:

- valid complaint
- wrong scale of solution
- external grooming / pressure
- catastrophe born from sincerity plus centralization

## Izack Rule

Izack is currently best handled as:

- alive
- present
- load-bearing
- deeper in the architecture than an ordinary person can remain
- unavailable in ordinary social ways, but not erased

He is not a dead ghost-note.
He is not a founder who could simply walk in and solve the plot.

He should feel like:

- active influence
- intermittent trace
- increasing system-depth
- late direct encounter with cost

## Process Rule

When adding lore, ask:

1. Does this help a scene feel lived in?
2. Does this deepen a relationship?
3. Does this create better tension, humor, or texture?
4. Does this support one of the three layers above?

If the answer is `no`, it probably belongs in notes, not the manuscript.

## Import Discipline

Use old lore files for:

- atmosphere
- rituals
- family memory
- geography
- ecology
- side-character texture
- scene ideas

Do not use old lore files as automatic authority for:

- current canon names
- chapter sequence
- who appears when
- live-book stakes

The live manuscript stays in charge.

## Langues Metric Rule

The Langues Metric is the mathematical heart of the Six Tongues system:

`L(x, t) = sum_{l=1}^6 w_l * exp(beta_l * (d_l + sin(omega_l*t + phi_l)))`

What this means narratively:

- **Six Sacred Tongues** (KO, AV, RU, CA, UM, DR) each have a weight based on the golden ratio
  - KO = 1.00, AV = 1.62, RU = 2.62, CA = 4.24, UM = 6.85, DR = 11.09
  - Higher tongue = more authority = more expensive to invoke = more dangerous if misused
- **Deviation d** from trusted reference increases cost **exponentially** — this is the core innovation
  - Small drift near safe operation = small cost
  - Large drift = exponentially catastrophic cost
  - Adversaries pay more the further they deviate
- **Temporal breathing** (the sin term) means the system pulses — it is not static
  - Breathing makes the tongues cycle between vigilant and permissive states
  - This is the 0.3-second Protocol heartbeat Marcus hears in ch01
- **Flux coefficients** (nu) control how many tongues are active at once
  - Polly state (nu >= 0.9): all tongues fully engaged — crisis mode
  - Collapsed state (nu < 0.1): tongue effectively dormant — this is what happened to UM in ch20 (Shardfold gap)

In the manuscript, the Langues Metric appears as:

- the Protocol's 0.3-second pulse (ch01)
- phi-weighting discovered by Marcus (ch16 — the fractal proof)
- the six-tongue witness formation in battle (ch06, ch07)
- the cost of invoking higher tongues (DR requires more attestation than KO)
- the gap in governance at Shardfold where UM is absent (ch20)

Do NOT explain the formula on-page. Let Marcus notice its effects — the exponential cost, the breathing, the weight asymmetry — through his detection faculty.

## Everweave Source Rule

The Everweave campaign log (528 pages, Foundation Era) is the original D&D transcript. Use it for:
- atmosphere, family texture, Izack's character voice
- sensory anchors: Fizzlecress (honeyed mint, fizzing apple cider), Cassisivadan pipe, crystal helix chamber
- Ame's pact details, Crystal Garden, Polly's bond arc, Clayborn's creation, Zara's apprenticeship

Do NOT use it as:
- authority over the live isekai's names, timeline, or chapter sequence
- proof that every old character or event belongs in this book
- a source for direct-import plot beats (the isekai translates the Everweave soul, it does not replay it)

## Seventh Silence Rule

The seventh silence is NOT:

- a seventh tongue
- a surprise new magic school
- a power-up

The seventh silence IS:

- relational architecture
- a domain for endings, transitions, acknowledgment
- the gaps between the six tongues
- what the Bonecasters have been holding with skulls and the system should be holding with structure

Izack has been holding the space for it manually for decades. That's why he's tired.
Marcus will implement it as a function of the existing six, not a replacement.

## Dominion Rule

The Dominion is NOT:

- cartoon villain
- demon army
- brute-force invader

The Dominion IS:

- strategic patient enemy using real system flaws as crowbar
- planted handlers around Kael (the "bad kids") over two years
- their strategy: convince someone inside to centralize → keys dissolve → door opens
- they don't need to steal anything — they need the system to believe unity is safer than consensus

The Dominion is dangerous because parts of their critique are true.

## Current North Star

The target book is:

- emotionally warm enough that the family and crew matter
- adventurous enough that the travel and conflict move
- magical enough that Pollyoneth feels alive
- technical enough that Marcus's programmer mind gives the world a second readable layer
- politically sharp enough that architecture, grief, and power all point at the same choice:
  distribute authority or open the door

The natural conclusion is:

- Marcus meets Izack in the deep observatory (ch27)
- learns to implement the seventh silence (function of existing six, not new power)
- receives Izack's request: "Bring him home" (Kael)
- ascends knowing what to build
- Book 1 ending: the audit is over, the work begins
- closes the audit arc, opens the door for Book 2 (implementing seventh silence, bringing Kael home, facing Dominion directly)

That is already a good book.
Polish and careful expansion are the right process now.
