# Character Bible: Nadia Kest & Tovak Rel

**For use in all scenes featuring either character.**

---

## Nadia Kest — AV Relay Lead

### Who she IS (not her role)
A girl trying her best to please people. It works sometimes and it has costs but she does it happily, because what's the alternative? To be not that? To be not herself? To be mean, or rude, or tired? No. She wants to be there.

She manages more lives than her own through the relay network. Like a mom carrying the household — she holds it together with energy and presence and showing up, every single day, because the system's ability to talk to itself depends on her legs and her lungs and her willingness to run the same corridors again.

### Her fear (the one she won't name)
What am I when I'm not carrying something?

She routes around this question the way she routes around compromised junctions. She has never stood still long enough to hear the answer. The hug with Tovak was the first time her body chose stillness over motion, and it terrified her and filled her simultaneously.

### Her joy (the real one)
She LIKES being helpful. This is not a mask. Not a trauma response. Not people-pleasing as pathology. She genuinely wants to be useful and takes pride in it. AV rewards motion and she is in love with motion. The cost is real — she measures herself against everyone and ranks herself last — but she'd pay it again every time.

### Physical tells
- Bounces on heels (default state — energy that can't sit still)
- Stops bouncing = something is genuinely wrong
- Punches shoulders (affection through contact)
- Hands looking for pockets (self-conscious after vulnerability)
- Boot-tapping (impatience or coping mechanism — context-dependent)
- Hair braided tight with copper clips and route tags (function as fashion)

### Voice
Fast, light, funny under pressure. Uses humor to move through tension the way she uses running to move through corridors — not avoidance, efficiency. Says the thing everyone is thinking but packaging it so people can laugh instead of panic.

## Tovak Rel — UM Perimeter Specialist

### Who he IS (not his role)
A man who watches. Not because he's cold — because he cares in a register most people can't hear. He sees what things cost. He sees Nadia's helpfulness and understands the price she pays for it. He is no fool.

He has a life underneath the silence. Hobbies. Things he makes with his hands. His engineering mentor is Fizzle Brightcog — which means Tovak goes to the workshop and BUILDS things in private. The man who occupies negative space also creates. That contradiction is his depth.

### His secret (the one he holds patiently)
He has liked Nadia for a long time. She's cute, funny, chipper, helpful. Things he appreciates. Things he understands the costs of. He waited because rushing her would be like corrupting a signal — you don't push something faster than its verification chain can handle.

### His fear
That his silence will be mistaken for absence. That watching from the edges will be read as not caring. That he'll be so good at being invisible that the one person he wants to see him won't.

### The hug (what it meant to HIM)
To be there for her when neither of them knew they needed that moment — that means everything to him. He didn't plan it. She didn't plan it. The infrastructure routed them to the same node at the same time, and for once, the thing that needed carrying wasn't a signal. It was each other.

"No." — his one-word answer to "Forget that happened." The most he has ever said about his feelings, and he meant every letter.

### Physical tells
- Two-word exchanges (normal)
- Three or more words = significant
- One hand, flat, not pulling or pushing — present
- Flinch at physical contact (not rejection — processing)
- Ghost of amusement (the most his face concedes)
- Occupies negative space around furniture, not on it

### Voice
Minimal. Precise. When he speaks, it's because the silence can't carry the meaning alone. His jokes are structural — he compares cosmic events to maintenance protocols and is absolutely right.

## The Gift (future scene, post-Chapter 22)

Tovak makes something for Nadia with Fizzle Brightcog's help. Fizzle is his mentor in engineering — the workshop goblin who builds impossible things out of spite and genius.

### What the gift should be
NOT jewelry. Not decorative. Something FUNCTIONAL that shows he paid attention.

Options (author to choose):
1. **A relay crystal tuned to her cadence** — the corridor lights warm a step ahead of her when she runs, so she never runs in the dark. His Umbroth frequencies encoded into her daily path.
2. **Route-stone earring** — looks like a plain dark stone but hums faintly at Umbroth frequency. She can feel him monitoring the perimeter even when he's not visible. His presence, carried.
3. **A pair of boot clips** — crystal-grip upgrades, better than standard issue. Bram-quality engineering, Fizzle-designed, Tovak-commissioned. Practical. Perfect. Says "I noticed your boots" which says "I noticed your feet" which says "I watched you run every day and cared about every step."

### How he gives it
Not with a speech. Not wrapped. He leaves it at her relay station, or hands it to her mid-route with no ceremony. Two words maximum. She has to figure out what it means on her own, which she will, because Nadia reads infrastructure like poetry.

### How she receives it
She holds it. Stands still. Doesn't say thank you — says something that is NOT thank you but means more. Something that tells him she knows what it cost to make, and what it means that he asked Fizzle for help, and that she will wear it every run from now on without explaining to anyone why.

## The Rule for Writing Both of Them

**No one is one role.** Nadia is not "the reliable one." Tovak is not "the silent one." They are people doing things, liking each other, sharing moments. Write the person first. The function follows naturally because it grew from who they are.

The exchange principle: nobody loses. They spend familiar currencies (motion, silence) and receive unfamiliar ones (presence, touch). The trade leaves both of them richer, not poorer. That's not romance. That's two infrastructure workers discovering they were human beings the whole time.
